Meanwhile, the Stanford HAI AI Index 2026 confirms China has nearly closed the US lead in model quality, even as US private investment dwarfs China's by 23x.

## Notable Papers

**The AI Scientist-v2: Workshop-Level Automated Scientific Discovery via Agentic Tree Search**
*Yamada, Lange et al. — Sakana AI / UBC / Oxford*
The system became the first AI to produce a fully autonomous scientific paper accepted through rigorous human peer review (ICLR workshop, scoring above 55% of human-authored submissions), and a companion paper describing the work was published in *Nature*. The system autonomously proposes hypotheses, runs experiments, analyzes data, and writes manuscripts end-to-end.
[arXiv:2504.08066](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08066) | [Nature coverage](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00934-w)

**Transformer Forecast Collapse Under Squared Loss (Andreoletti, 2026)**
A formal proof showing Transformer-based models collapse to trivial forecasts under squared loss in specific stochastic environments — a significant theoretical finding on architectural limits of the dominant model family.
[arXiv — cs.AI/recent](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent)

**Stanford HAI AI Index 2026**
The ninth annual report finds: SWE-bench Verified scores jumped from 60% to nearly 100% in one year; frontier models now match or exceed human baselines on PhD-level science and competition math; yet the same models read analog clocks correctly only 50.1% of the time. China has nearly eliminated the US performance gap despite 23x less private investment. Foundation model transparency scores dropped from 58 to 40 points year-over-year.
[Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index) | [The Decoder summary](https://the-decoder.com/stanfords-ai-index-2026-shows-rapid-progress-growing-safety-concerns-and-declining-public-trust/)

## Model & Product Launches

### **Claude Mythos Preview — Anthropic** *(Special Watchlist)*
Anthropic announced Claude Mythos Preview on April 7 — its most powerful model to date and a new model class. It scored **93.9% on SWE-bench Verified** (vs. Claude Opus 4.6's 80.8%) and demonstrated the ability to discover thousands of previously unknown zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS, browser, and widely-used open-source software — including a flaw in OpenBSD that had gone undetected for 27 years. **Anthropic declined a public release**, instead making it available exclusively to a curated group of ~40 companies (Amazon, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorgan Chase, the Linux Foundation, Microsoft, Palo Alto Networks, and others) via a new initiative called **Project Glasswing**. It is also accessible through Amazon Bedrock and Google Cloud Vertex AI as a gated research preview.
[TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/07/anthropic-mythos-ai-model-preview-security/) | [Anthropic/Glasswing](https://www.anthropic.com/glasswing) | [AISI evaluation](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/our-evaluation-of-claude-mythos-previews-cyber-capabilities) | [InfoQ](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/04/anthropic-claude-mythos/)

### **Meta Muse Spark**
Released April 8, Muse Spark is the first model from Meta Superintelligence Labs — built over nine months under Alexandr Wang (code-named "Avocado"). It's natively multimodal (voice, text, image in; text out), supports tool-use and multi-agent orchestration, and scores 52 on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index (behind only Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.4, and Claude Opus 4.6). **Notably, it is proprietary** — a significant break from Meta's open-source Llama tradition. It currently powers the Meta AI app and will roll out to WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, and Messenger.
[Meta AI blog](https://ai.meta.com/blog/introducing-muse-spark-msl/) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/08/meta-debuts-the-muse-spark-model-in-a-ground-up-overhaul-of-its-ai/) | [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/08/meta-debuts-first-major-ai-model-since-14-billion-deal-to-bring-in-alexandr-wang.html)

### **Microsoft MAI Models (MAI-Transcribe-1, MAI-Voice-1, MAI-Image-2)**
Launched April 2, Microsoft's in-house trio covers speech-to-text (2.5× faster than Azure Fast, 25 languages), voice generation (60 seconds of audio in 1 second, custom voices), and image generation (top-3 on Arena.ai leaderboard). All available through Microsoft Foundry and a new MAI Playground — a deliberate move to reduce OpenAI dependency.
[Microsoft AI](https://microsoft.ai/news/today-were-announcing-3-new-world-class-mai-models-available-in-foundry/) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/02/microsoft-takes-on-ai-rivals-with-three-new-foundational-models/) | [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/microsoft-launches-3-new-ai-models-in-direct-shot-at-openai-and-google)

### **Google Gemma 4**
Released April 2 under Apache 2.0, Gemma 4 delivers frontier-level performance at substantially lower compute cost than Llama 4 Maverick (400B parameters), according to early benchmarks.

## Key Opinions & Statements

### Sam Altman — "The Gentle Singularity" + Security + Industrial Policy
Three significant moments from Altman this week:
1. **"The Gentle Singularity"** blog post: Altman argues 2026 will see AI systems capable of generating novel scientific insights, and that the quality-of-life gains from AI-accelerated science will be transformative. [blog.samaltman.com](https://blog.samaltman.com/the-gentle-singularity)
2. **Response to attack on his home**: A Molotov cocktail was thrown at Altman's San Francisco residence in early April. In a public response, he described himself as "a flawed person in the center of an exceptionally complex situation" and argued the right answer to the "ring of power" problem in AI is broad distribution of the technology — not concentration. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/11/sam-altman-responds-to-incendiary-new-yorker-article-after-attack-on-his-home/)
3. **OpenAI goals for automated research**: A recent livestream recap on X revealed OpenAI's internal targets — an automated AI **research intern** by September 2026 (running on hundreds of thousands of GPUs) and a true **automated AI researcher** by March 2028. [X/sama](https://x.com/sama/status/1983584366547829073)

### Andrej Karpathy — LLM Wiki & AI Power User Gap
Two notable ideas from Karpathy this week:
1. **"Living Wiki" over RAG**: Karpathy published a pattern for LLM-maintained knowledge bases, arguing RAG is flawed because it "rediscovers everything from scratch on every query." His alternative — structured Markdown files that AI iteratively builds and refines — garnered 5,000+ GitHub stars within days. [Analytics Vidhya](https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/blog/2026/04/llm-wiki-by-andrej-karpathy/)
2. **AI power user divide**: On April 10, Karpathy warned of a widening split between AI power users and skeptics who are "speaking past each other" due to diverging mental models and tooling fluency. [Let's Data Science](https://letsdatascience.com/news/karpathy-warns-growing-gap-among-ai-users-a6adb56d)

### Demis Hassabis — On Commercial Pressure & the Path to AGI
In a recent 20VC appearance, Hassabis described the sector as locked in a "ferocious commercial pressure race" amplified by US-China geopolitical tensions. He reiterated that the next gains toward AGI will come from algorithmic breakthroughs in continual learning, memory, world models, and hybrid systems — not just scale — and pegged AGI arrival at roughly 2030–2035. [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/google-deepmind-hassabis-startup-pace) | [36kr/en](https://eu.36kr.com/en/p/3760954475000322)

### Yann LeCun — Left Meta, Launched AMI Labs, Still Anti-LLM
In a Brown University lecture (April 1), LeCun doubled down on his position: *"There's literally hundreds of billions invested in an industry that basically is counting on the fact that LLMs are going to reach human-level intelligence. It's complete BS."* His slide deck included: "IF YOU ARE INTERESTED IN HUMAN-LEVEL AI, DON'T WORK ON LLMs." LeCun has since left Meta and launched **AMI Labs**, which closed over **$1 billion** in funding to pursue a world-model-based approach to AI. [Brown Daily Herald](https://www.browndailyherald.com/article/2026/04/ai-pioneer-yann-lecun-discusses-new-frontiers-in-the-field-at-brown-lecture/) | [Christian & Timbers](https://www.christianandtimbers.com/insights/what-yann-lecuns-1-billion-bet-reveals-about-ai-leadership-in-2026)
