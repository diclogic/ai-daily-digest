## Notable Papers

### AI Co-Mathematician: Accelerating Mathematicians with Agentic AI
**Authors:** Google DeepMind | **Published:** May 7, 2026

A multi-agent system built on Gemini 3.1 Pro that collaborates with human mathematicians on open research problems. Scored 48% on FrontierMath Tier 4 (up from 19% for the base model alone), setting a new high among all AI systems on problems "designed to remain unsolved by AI for decades." Oxford mathematician Marc Lackenby used the system to resolve an open problem from the Kourovka Notebook (Problem 21.10 in group theory).

[arXiv paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06651)

### Manifold Steering: Neural Network Geometry for Behavioral Control
**Authors:** Stanford University researchers

Demonstrates that the intrinsic geometry of neural network representations is tightly coupled with model behavior. Proposes "manifold steering" as a geometry-aware intervention yielding 2.8x improvement in naturalness over conventional linear steering across language and vision tasks.

### On-Policy Self-Distillation for LLM Training
Combines on-policy relevance of reinforcement learning with dense token-level feedback, achieving 70% on the AIME'24 math benchmark with Qwen3-8B while delivering a 30x cost reduction compared to off-policy distillation.

## Model & Product Launches

### GPT-5.5 Instant — Free Tier Rollout (May 5)
OpenAI released GPT-5.5 Instant to all free-tier ChatGPT users. The model produces 52.5% fewer hallucinations than GPT-5.3 Instant on high-stakes prompts (medicine, law, finance) and pulls context from prior chats, saved files, and connected Gmail. Sam Altman noted the model "planned its own launch party for May 5 at 5:55 PM."

[OpenAI announcement](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-instant/)

### Claude Mythos Preview — Cybersecurity Dominance
Anthropic's Claude Mythos Preview remains in gated access with ~40 organizations (Apple, Amazon, JPMorgan Chase, Palo Alto Networks). Dario Amodei warned that Mythos has exposed "tens of thousands of software vulnerabilities" and that adversary AI models are only "six to 12 months" behind, creating a narrow window to patch critical infrastructure. Anthropic's Project Glasswing is using Mythos to proactively secure high-priority software.

[Anthropic Project Glasswing](https://www.anthropic.com/glasswing) | [CNBC coverage](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/08/anthropic-mythos-ai-cybersecurity-banks.html)

### Chinese Open-Weight Model Wave
Four Chinese labs released open-weight coding models within a 12-day window: Z.ai's GLM-5.1, MiniMax M2.7, Moonshot's Kimi K2.6, and **DeepSeek V4**. DeepSeek V4-Pro (1.6T parameters, 1M context) charges $3.48/M output tokens vs. $25–30 at Western frontier labs, with performance "rivaling the world's top closed-source models." Released under MIT License with Huawei Ascend 950 chip integration.

In a notable change from his previous warnings about white-collar job displacement, Amodei is now publicly entertaining Jevons Paradox — the idea that AI could create more demand and more jobs, not fewer. This marks a significant rhetorical shift for the Anthropic CEO.

[Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/05/dario-amodei-jevons-paradox-will-ai-wipe-out-white-collar-jobs/)

### Yann LeCun — "AI Doom Narratives Are Extremely Destructive"
Now leading AMI Labs ($1.03B seed round, largest in European history), LeCun told Axios that predictions of 20% AI job losses are "ridiculously stupid" and are pushing teenagers into depression and bad career decisions. "Everyone is going to be a boss," he said — "you'll manage agents instead of people." AMI Labs is built around JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture), betting that LLMs alone will never reach human-level intelligence.

[Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/05/ai-job-apocalypse-warnings-destructive-yann-lecun/) | [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/04/ai-godfather-survival-guide-hype-doom)

### Andrej Karpathy — Agentic Engineering and the "Slopacolypse"
At Sequoia's AI Ascent 2026, Karpathy outlined a framework for AI-driven software development: "vibe coding" (anyone can build by describing what they want), "agentic engineering" (agents that preserve professional quality), and "Software 3.0." He also warned 2026 will be the year of the "slopacolypse" — poor-quality AI-generated content flooding digital media. His open-source AutoResearch project (630 lines, MIT License) continues gaining traction, with 21,000+ GitHub stars.

[Cybernews](https://cybernews.com/ai-news/andrej-karpathy-slopacolypse/) | [GitHub](https://github.com/karpathy/autoresearch)
