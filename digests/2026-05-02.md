## Notable Papers & Research

**AlphaEvolve: A Gemini-Powered Coding Agent for Designing Advanced Algorithms (Google DeepMind)**
An evolutionary coding agent that pairs Gemini's creative problem-solving with automated evaluators in an evolutionary loop. AlphaEvolve discovered a faster matrix multiplication algorithm improving on Strassen's 1969 result, raised the kissing number lower bound in 11 dimensions, and found a data-center scheduling heuristic that recovers 0.7% of Google's global compute continuously. Now being integrated into Google's TPU chip design pipeline.
[Google DeepMind Blog](https://deepmind.google/blog/alphaevolve-a-gemini-powered-coding-agent-for-designing-advanced-algorithms/) | [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13131)

**RecursiveMAS: Recursive Computation in Multi-Agent Systems**
A framework that enables AI agents to refine collaborative reasoning through iterative latent-space interactions rather than explicit text exchange. Achieves average accuracy improvements of up to 20.2% and inference speedups of up to 2.4x on multi-agent benchmarks.
[arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent)

**TurboQuant: KV Cache Compression (Google, ICLR 2026)**
An algorithm that dramatically reduces the memory overhead from KV caches — one of the biggest bottlenecks in running large models with massive context windows. Combines PolarQuant vector rotation with quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss compression, with significant implications for on-device AI and data center efficiency.

## Key Opinions & Statements

**Dario Amodei (Anthropic CEO)**
The Pentagon finalized classified AI deals with seven tech companies on May 1, conspicuously excluding Anthropic, which remains designated a "supply-chain risk" — a label previously reserved for companies associated with foreign adversaries. The blacklisting stems from Amodei's refusal to remove contractual bans on Claude's use for mass domestic surveillance or fully autonomous weapons. Anthropic sued the Trump administration, and a federal judge in California blocked the government's action, but the DOD continues to enforce the designation. White House discussions with Amodei reopened in April, and Trump told CNBC a deal is "possible." Separately, Anthropic continues to weigh a $50B funding round at a $900B valuation.
[CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/01/tech/pentagon-ai-anthropic) | [Defense News](https://www.defensenews.com/news/pentagon-congress/2026/05/01/pentagon-freezes-out-anthropic-as-it-signs-deals-with-ai-rivals/)

**Sam Altman (OpenAI CEO)**
The Musk v. OpenAI trial wrapped its first week in Oakland federal court. Key revelations: OpenAI lawyers alleged Shivon Zilis, a longtime Musk employee and mother to four of his children, acted as a covert liaison feeding OpenAI information to Musk after he left the board in 2018. Musk admitted under cross-examination that xAI used OpenAI's models for distillation, calling it "standard practice." Musk also conceded he "did not read the fine print" about OpenAI's for-profit conversion. The judge admonished Musk for posting about the case on X and for dramatic AI safety rhetoric ("We could all die"). Trial continues through late May, with testimony from Satya Nadella and Ilya Sutskever expected. Separately, seven families of Tumbler Ridge shooting victims filed a $1B lawsuit against OpenAI, alleging the company failed to alert police after flagging the shooter's ChatGPT account for "gun violence activity and planning" in June 2025.
