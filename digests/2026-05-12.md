## Notable Papers & Research

**Manifold Steering for Neural Network Control** — Stanford University
Proposes "manifold steering," a geometry-aware intervention for controlling neural network behavior that yields 2.8x average improvement in naturalness across language and vision tasks compared to conventional linear steering. Shows that the intrinsic geometry of neural network representations is tightly coupled with model behavior.

**SkillOS: Experience-Driven RL for LLM Agents** — UIUC & Google Cloud AI Research
Introduces an experience-driven reinforcement learning framework that enables LLM agents to automatically curate reusable skills, improving sample efficiency and generalization in multi-step agentic tasks. ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

**Google DeepMind AI Co-Mathematician**
An agent-based workbench for mathematical research that achieved a new high score on the FrontierMath Tier 4 benchmark, solving 23 out of 48 problems — the hardest tier of open mathematical problems.

**Agentic AI Orchestration Should Be Bayes-Consistent** — Position Paper (May 4)
Argues that the control layer of agentic AI systems must be grounded in Bayesian principles to maintain calibrated beliefs, leading to better resource allocation and more reliable task execution. ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

**SmolDocling** — 256M Parameter Vision-Language Model
A compact model that performs end-to-end document conversion with robust performance across various document types, demonstrating that small models can achieve strong results on structured document understanding. ([Hugging Face Trending](https://huggingface.co/papers/trending))

## Key Opinions & Statements

### Dario Amodei — Cybersecurity "Moment of Danger"
Amodei warned that Mythos has created a narrow window to fix decades-old software vulnerabilities before adversaries gain similar capabilities. He noted Chinese AI models are "maybe six to 12 months behind," giving roughly that amount of time to patch critical flaws. Separately, he invoked the **Jevons Paradox** on AI employment: "If you automate 90% of the job, then everyone does the 10% of the job" — a notable shift from his earlier warnings about potential white-collar job elimination. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/05/anthropic-ceo-cyber-moment-of-danger-mythos-vulnerabilities.html)) ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/05/dario-amodei-jevons-paradox-will-ai-wipe-out-white-collar-jobs/))

### Andrej Karpathy — "From Vibe Coding to Agentic Engineering"
At **Sequoia AI Ascent 2026**, Karpathy framed the current transition: "vibe coding" lowered the floor so non-technical people can build software, but **agentic engineering** is about using AI agents while preserving professional quality. He argued that as AI improves, human understanding becomes *more* critical, not less — developers are becoming "editors, reviewers, and system designers" while models handle implementation. He also cautioned: "We're still very much in the realm of pattern matching, and we need to bridge the gap towards true reasoning." ([Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/ec/podcast/andrej-karpathy-at-sequoia-ai-ascent-2026-from-vibe/id1799918505?i=1000765806763&l=en-GB))

### Yann LeCun — AI Job Doom Narratives Are "Extremely Destructive"
Now executive chairman of **AMI Labs** (his $1B-funded post-Meta venture), LeCun pushed back forcefully on AI employment fears: CEO warnings about AI job loss are "extremely destructive" and people should "listen to economists rather than CEOs" who have a vested interest in hyping their products. He stressed that historically, new technologies take ~15 years to achieve their productivity promise. ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/05/ai-job-apocalypse-warnings-destructive-yann-lecun/)) ([Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/04/ai-godfather-survival-guide-hype-doom))

## Other Notable AI News

### Musk v. Altman Trial — Week 3 Begins
The landmark trial entered its third week in Oakland federal court. **Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella** testified on May 11 that Musk never contacted him with concerns about Microsoft's OpenAI investments — not at the 2019 partnership announcement, the 2020 GPT-3 license, or the 2023 $10B investment. Nadella noted he and Musk have each other's phone numbers. **Sam Altman is expected to take the stand Tuesday.** Board chairman Bret Taylor is currently testifying. The jury could begin deliberations by Thursday. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/11/microsoft-ceo-satya-nadella-musk-altman-trial.html)) ([MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/05/08/1137008/musk-v-altman-week-2-openai-fires-back-and-shivon-zilis-reveals-that-musk-tried-to-poach-sam-altman/))

### Anthropic's 80x Growth and SpaceX Data Center Deal
Anthropic reported **80-fold annualized growth** in Q1 2026, reaching a **$30B revenue run rate** — driven largely by enterprise adoption of Claude Code by companies including Uber and Netflix. To cope with demand, Anthropic signed a deal to rent all AI capacity at SpaceX's **Colossus 1** data center in Memphis: 220,000 NVIDIA GPUs and 300+ megawatts of power. The unlikely partnership with Musk underscores the severity of the compute crunch. ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/08/anthropic-80fold-growth-quarter-renting-elon-musk-data-center/)) ([VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-says-it-hit-a-30-billion-revenue-run-rate-after-crazy-80x-growth))
