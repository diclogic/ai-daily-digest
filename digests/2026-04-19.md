## Notable Papers & Research

### The AI Scientist-v2: Workshop-Level Automated Scientific Discovery via Agentic Tree Search
**Authors:** Yamada, Lange et al. (Sakana AI) — [arXiv:2504.08066](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08066)

An end-to-end agentic system that autonomously formulates hypotheses, designs experiments, analyzes data, and writes scientific manuscripts. Notably, one fully AI-generated paper exceeded the average human acceptance threshold at an ICLR workshop — the first instance of a fully AI-generated paper passing peer review.

### The Ideation Bottleneck: Decomposing the Quality Gap Between AI-Generated and Human Economics Research
[arXiv:2604.03338](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03338)

Decomposes the quality gap between AI-generated and human-authored economics research into idea quality vs. execution quality, finding that autonomous AI systems can now generate complete research papers but still lag humans on research ideation.

### Forecast Collapse of Transformer-Based Models Under Squared Loss
[arXiv:2604.00064](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00064)

A formal proof showing that Transformer-based time-series forecasting models collapse to predicting the mean under squared loss — a significant theoretical result with practical implications for the rapidly growing AI forecasting space.

### Stanford AI Index Report 2026
[Full report](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report) — [IEEE Spectrum coverage](https://spectrum.ieee.org/state-of-ai-index-2026)

Key findings: Frontier models now exceed human baselines on PhD-level science questions; SWE-bench Verified scores jumped from 60% to nearly 100% in a single year; global corporate AI investment hit $581.7B in 2025 (+130% YoY); organizational adoption reached 88%; but public trust declined, with only 23% of the U.S. public viewing AI's job impact positively. Documented AI incidents rose to 362 (up from 233 in 2024).

## Model & Product Launches

### Claude Opus 4.7 — Anthropic (April 16)
[CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/16/anthropic-claude-opus-4-7-model-mythos.html) · [GitHub Changelog](https://github.blog/changelog/2026-04-16-claude-opus-4-7-is-generally-available/)

Anthropic's most powerful generally available model. Key upgrades include high-resolution image support (2576px / 3.75MP), a new "xhigh" effort level, task budgets (token-aware agentic loops), and the /ultrareview multi-agent code review feature. Outperforms Opus 4.6 on agentic coding, multidisciplinary reasoning, and scaled tool use. Same pricing: $5/$25 per million input/output tokens.

### Claude Mythos Preview & Project Glasswing — Anthropic (April 7)
[The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/04/anthropics-claude-mythos-finds.html) · [Help Net Security](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/04/08/anthropic-claude-mythos-preview-identify-vulnerabilities/) · [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/07/anthropic-claude-mythos-model-project-glasswing-cybersecurity/)

Mythos autonomously discovered thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and browser, including a 27-year-old OpenBSD bug and a 17-year-old FreeBSD RCE (CVE-2026-4747). **Anthropic withheld Mythos from general release** due to cybersecurity risks and instead formed Project Glasswing — a consortium of AWS, Apple, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, and others — to use the model defensively. The CFR called it ["an inflection point for AI and global security."](https://www.cfr.org/articles/six-reasons-claude-mythos-is-an-inflection-point-for-ai-and-global-security)

### Muse Spark — Meta (April 8)
[TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/08/meta-debuts-the-muse-spark-model-in-a-ground-up-overhaul-of-its-ai/) · [Meta AI Blog](https://ai.meta.com/blog/introducing-muse-spark-msl/)

Meta's first proprietary model, built by Meta Superintelligence Labs under new chief AI officer Alexandr Wang. A natively multimodal reasoning model with visual chain-of-thought, tool use, and multi-agent orchestration ("Contemplating mode"). Scores 52 on the Intelligence Index (vs. Claude Opus 4.6 at 53, Gemini 3.1 Pro at 57). Over an order of magnitude more efficient than Llama 4 Maverick. Rolling out across Meta AI, WhatsApp, Instagram, and Facebook.

### GPT-6 / "Spud" — OpenAI (Imminent)
[FindSkill.ai](https://findskill.ai/blog/gpt-6-release-date/)

Pre-training completed March 24 at the Stargate data centre. Sam Altman said launch is "a few weeks" away, but as of April 19 no official announcement has been made. Polymarket puts "GPT-6 by April 30" odds at ~72%. The official name is reportedly still undecided (GPT-5.5 is the "insider lean").

### Gemma 4 — Google DeepMind (April 2)
[Google Blog](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/gemma-4/) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/blog/gemma4)

Four variants (2.3B–31B parameters) released under Apache 2.0, with 256K context, native vision and audio, 140+ languages, and fully offline operation on edge devices. Over 400 million cumulative Gemma downloads to date.
