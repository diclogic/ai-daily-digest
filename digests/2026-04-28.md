## Notable Papers

- **Hyperloop Transformers** — MIT. Introduces a looped Transformer architecture where only a "middle cycle" block repeats, combined with hyper-connections that expand the residual stream. Achieves lower perplexity than depth-matched ordinary Transformers at 240M, 1B, and 2B parameters while using ~50% fewer parameters. [arXiv:2604.21254](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21254)

- **Thinking with Reasoning Skills (TRS)** — Multi-university collaboration. A training-free, retrieval-augmented framework that distills long chain-of-thought deliberation into compact "reasoning skill cards" with explicit triggers and pitfalls. Retrieved at inference time, these cards guide LLMs along direct solution paths, significantly reducing token usage and cost while matching or improving accuracy on math and coding benchmarks (AIME 2024–2026, HMMT). [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.21764v1)

- **ICLR 2026 concludes today** in Rio de Janeiro (April 24–28), with keynotes spanning core ML, robotics, neuroscience, and AI for science. Notable accepted work includes the AI Scientist-v2 autonomous research system, which had a fully AI-generated paper accepted at a major ML workshop — a first. [ICLR 2026](https://iclr.cc/)

### Demis Hassabis — Google AI Campus in Seoul, AGI by 2030
Hassabis met with South Korean President Lee Jae-myung on April 27 and announced the world's first Google AI Campus, opening in Seoul's Gangnam district by end of 2026. Google will dispatch at least 10 top researchers from its US headquarters to work with Korean startups, academics, and government teams. The campus will anchor Korea's "K-Moonshot Project," targeting 12 grand challenges across biotech, quantum, semiconductors, and energy by 2035. Hassabis projected AGI could materialize as early as 2030 and presented President Lee with a Go board signed by himself and Lee Se-dol, marking the 10th anniversary of the AlphaGo match. ## Other Notable AI News

- **China blocks Meta's $2B Manus acquisition.** China's National Development and Reform Commission ordered Meta to unwind its takeover of Manus, a Singaporean AI agent startup with Chinese roots. Meta had already integrated Manus into its internal systems and onboarded its executives. The move signals that cross-border AI M&A is now squarely a national-security issue for Beijing, and could chill future US-China tech deals. Meta says the transaction "complied fully with applicable law." [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/27/meta-manus-china-blocks-acquisition-ai-startup.html) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/27/china-vetoes-metas-2b-manus-deal-after-months-long-probe/) | [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/27/g-s1-118892/china-blocks-meta-from-acquiring-ai-startup-manus)

- **MemPalace crosses 19K GitHub stars.** The open-source AI memory system by Milla Jovovich, which gives LLMs persistent cross-session memory using verbatim storage and vector search (no summarization), continues rapid adoption since its April 5 launch. Runs entirely locally on ChromaDB and SQLite with zero API costs. Claims the highest LongMemEval benchmark score (96.6% raw), though some methodology choices have been questioned by independent reviewers. [GitHub](https://github.com/milla-jovovich/mempalace) | [mempalace.tech](https://www.mempalace.tech/)

- **Sereact raises $110M for robotic reasoning.** The Stuttgart-based startup builds AI models that help robots reason through tasks and predict consequences before acting. Series B led by Headline. Part of a broader surge in AI-native robotics funding in 2026.

- **AI legislative acceleration.** The US has gone from 6 to 25 signed AI laws since mid-March, with 78 chatbot-specific bills alive in 27 states. The White House's National Policy Framework recommends federal preemption to create a single national standard — a move that could simplify compliance but faces pushback from states with stricter rules. [Transparency Coalition](https://www.transparencycoalition.ai/news/ai-legislative-update-april24-2026)
