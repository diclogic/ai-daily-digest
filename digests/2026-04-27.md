## Notable Papers

- **The AI Scientist-v2: Workshop-Level Automated Scientific Discovery via Agentic Tree Search** — Introduces an autonomous research system that can propose hypotheses, design and run experiments, analyze results, and write full papers. A paper generated entirely by this system was recently accepted at a major ML workshop — a first. [arXiv (April 2026)](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

- **Crashing Waves vs. Rising Tides: AI Automation from Thousands of Worker Evaluations** — MIT Future of Tech. Evaluates 3,000+ labor tasks and finds that "rising tides" (continuous broad-based capability improvement) rather than "crashing waves" (abrupt surges) are the primary form of AI-driven automation, challenging narratives of sudden mass displacement. [MIT FutureTech](https://futuretech.mit.edu/publication/crashing-waves-vs-rising-tides-preliminary-findings-on-ai-automation-from-thousands-of-worker-evaluations-of-labor-market-tasks)

- **Uni-SafeBench: A Unified Safety Benchmark for Multimodal Large Models** — Proposes a comprehensive safety evaluation framework spanning text, image, audio, and video modalities, addressing a gap as models become increasingly multimodal. Finds that safety degrades significantly with non-text input modalities. [arXiv (April 2026)](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

## Model & Product Launches

### Google's $40B Anthropic Investment (April 24)
Google parent Alphabet will invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic — $10B in cash now at a $350B valuation, with an additional $30B contingent on Claude performance targets. This comes days after Amazon committed up to $25B. Combined with Anthropic's $30B+ ARR, the company has secured over 11GW of committed compute from Amazon (5GW Trainium), Google (5GW TPU), and Nvidia (1GW GPU). [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/24/google-to-invest-up-to-40-billion-in-anthropic-as-search-giant-spreads-its-ai-bets.html) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/24/google-to-invest-up-to-40b-in-anthropic-in-cash-and-compute/)

### GPT-5.5 — API Now Live (April 24)
GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.5 Pro are now available in the API after launching to ChatGPT subscribers on April 23. OpenAI calls it their "smartest and most intuitive" model, with particular strength in agentic workflows — writing code, researching online, operating software, and chaining tools until tasks are finished. More token-efficient than GPT-5.4 at higher intelligence, with matching per-token latency. [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/23/openai-chatgpt-gpt-5-5-ai-model-superapp/)

### DeepSeek V4 — Adoption Accelerating
DeepSeek V4-Pro (1.6T params, 49B active) and V4-Flash (284B params, 13B active) continue gaining traction since their April 24 launch. V4-Pro scores 80.6% on SWE-bench Verified — within 0.2 points of Claude Opus 4.6 — while Flash scores 79.0% at a fraction of the cost ($0.14/$0.28 per 1M tokens). Both models are open-weight under Apache 2.0 with a native 1M-token context window, using only 27% of V3.2's inference FLOPs and 10% of KV cache via a novel hybrid attention mechanism. [Artificial Analysis](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/deepseek-is-back-among-the-leading-open-weights-models-with-v4-pro-and-v4-flash) | [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro)

### Alibaba Qwen 3.6-Max-Preview
Alibaba released Qwen 3.6-Max-Preview, its most advanced model, with strong gains in coding, reasoning, and agentic performance. The Chinese AI race continues to intensify alongside DeepSeek and Tencent's Hy3 preview release.

## Key Opinions & Statements

### Andrej Karpathy — Autoresearch Hits 66K Stars
Karpathy's `autoresearch` project, released March 7, has exploded to 66,000+ GitHub stars and 9,600 forks. The tool runs ML experiments in an autonomous loop — modifying code, training for 5 minutes, keeping improvements, discarding regressions — with no human intervention. Shopify CEO Tobi Lutke reported a 19% validation score improvement from 37 experiments on a 0.8B-parameter model overnight. Karpathy's broader thesis: the human role is shifting from low-level coder to high-level research manager. [GitHub](https://github.com/karpathy/autoresearch) | [DataCamp](https://www.datacamp.com/tutorial/guide-to-autoresearch)

### Sam Altman — Trial Eve and Tumbler Ridge Fallout
The Musk v. Altman trial begins jury selection tomorrow (Monday) in Oakland federal court. $134B in damages at stake. Meanwhile, Canada's joint ISED–Public Safety task force is developing mandatory AI threat-reporting rules following Altman's apology for OpenAI's failure to alert law enforcement about the Tumbler Ridge shooter's ChatGPT account — an account banned 8 months before the February massacre that killed 8 people. [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/24/musk-v-altman-trial-openai-lawsuit-xai.html) | [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/4/25/chkopenaissamaltmanapologises-over-failure-to-report-canadian-mass-shooter)

### Yann LeCun — Dismisses Mythos Concerns, Pushes JEPA
LeCun dismissed growing concern over Claude Mythos Preview as "exaggerated and unnecessary drama," and continued his campaign against LLM-centric AI at a Brown University lecture. He also sharply criticized Dario Amodei's prediction that AI will displace 50% of entry-level jobs, calling it "destructive and dangerous." His new JEPA framework and LeWorldModel (~15M parameters, trainable on a single GPU) offer a concrete alternative to autoregressive language models. [Storyboard18](https://www.storyboard18.com/brand-makers/meta-ai-chief-yann-lecun-downplays-fears-over-anthropics-claude-mythos-94932.htm) | [StartupNews](https://startupnews.fyi/2026/04/19/ai-pioneer-yann-lecun-rejects-anthropics-50-percent-job-loss-prediction-destructive-and-dangerous/)
