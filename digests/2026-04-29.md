# AI Daily Digest — April 29, 2026

**Executive Summary:** The Musk v. OpenAI trial opened in Oakland with Elon Musk taking the stand seeking $130 billion in damages and Sam Altman's ouster, while Google quietly signed a classified AI deal with the Pentagon allowing Gemini use for "any lawful government purpose."

## Notable Papers

- **"The Reasoning Trap"** — ICLR 2026. Found that training models for stronger reasoning via reinforcement learning *increases* tool-hallucination rates in lockstep with task gains, challenging the assumption that better reasoning fixes hallucination. Partial mitigations (prompt engineering, DPO) help but don't close the gap. ([asanify.com](https://asanify.com/blog/news/ai-agent-hallucination-april-29-2026/))

- **GPTZero: 50+ Hallucinated Citations in ICLR 2026 Submissions** — Scanning 300 ICLR papers on OpenReview, 90 were flagged for citations that don't appear to exist online; after human verification, 50 papers contained at least one fabricated reference. ([gptzero.me](https://gptzero.me/news/iclr-2026/))

- **"The AI Scientist-v2"** — Sakana AI. An end-to-end agentic system that autonomously formulates hypotheses, runs experiments, and writes scientific manuscripts via agentic tree search. One fully AI-generated paper exceeded the average human acceptance threshold at an ICLR workshop — the first such instance. ([arXiv:2504.08066](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08066))

## Model & Product Launches

- **OpenAI GPT-5.5** (April 23) — Described as OpenAI's "smartest and most intuitive" model. Strong gains in agentic coding, computer use, and knowledge work. More token-efficient than GPT-5.4. Rolling out to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise tiers in ChatGPT and Codex; API access live as of April 24. ([openai.com](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/)) ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/23/openai-announces-latest-artificial-intelligence-model.html))

- **DeepSeek V4** (April 24) — Open-source MoE models trained entirely on domestic Chinese hardware (Huawei Ascend 950, Cambricon). V4-Pro: 1.6T parameters (49B activated), 1M-token context. V4-Flash: 284B parameters (13B activated). Competitive with Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.4 on benchmarks, at a fraction of the cost ($0.14/M input tokens for Flash). ([MIT Tech Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/04/24/1136422/why-deepseeks-v4-matters/)) ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/24/deepseek-v4-ai-model-price-performance-china-open-source/))

- **Claude Mythos Preview** (April 7, restricted) — Anthropic's most powerful model, withheld from public release due to its ability to autonomously find zero-day vulnerabilities at scale. Available only to ~50 organizations under Project Glasswing for defensive cybersecurity. The UK AISI independently confirmed Mythos succeeds 73% of the time on expert-level tasks no prior model could complete. A Discord group accessed it on launch day by guessing the preview URL via info from a third-party contractor. ([red.anthropic.com](https://red.anthropic.com/2026/mythos-preview/)) ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/23/anthropic-mythos-leak-dario-amodei-ceo-cybersecurity-hackers-exploits-ai/))

- **Meta Muse Spark** (April 8) — First model from Meta Superintelligence Labs (led by Alexandr Wang). Natively multimodal reasoning model with tool use, visual chain of thought, and multi-agent orchestration. Small and fast by design. Rolling out across WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, Messenger, and AI glasses. ([Meta AI blog](https://ai.meta.com/blog/introducing-muse-spark-msl/)) ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/08/meta-debuts-the-muse-spark-model-in-a-ground-up-overhaul-of-its-ai/))

- **Google Gemini 3.1 Pro** — Leads 13 of 16 major benchmarks and ties GPT-5.4 Pro on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index at roughly one-third the API cost. Gemma 4 also released under Apache 2.0. ([llm-stats.com](https://llm-stats.com/ai-news))

## Key Opinions & Statements

- **Elon Musk** testified at the opening of *Musk v. OpenAI* in Oakland, claiming he "came up with the idea, the name, recruited the key people, taught them everything I know, provided all the initial funding." He is seeking $130 billion in damages and wants Altman and Brockman removed from leadership, arguing they betrayed OpenAI's nonprofit mission. Cross-examination continues Wednesday. ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/28/nx-s1-5801438/musk-altman-openai-trial-opening-statements)) ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/28/tech/elon-musk-sam-altman-openai))

- **Dario Amodei** met with White House Chief of Staff Susie Wiles and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent to discuss Mythos capabilities. Separately, warned that a "substantial portion" of entry-level white-collar jobs in finance, consulting, and tech could be replaced by AI within 1-5 years. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/17/anthropic-dario-amodei-trump-mythos.html)) ([Atlanta Daily World](https://atlantadailyworld.com/2026/04/20/anthropic-ceo-warns-that-ai-could-displace-entry-level-roles-within-five-years/))

- **Demis Hassabis** visited South Korea and met with President Lee Jae Myung to discuss AI guardrails and basic income in an AI-reshaped society. Also met Samsung, SK hynix, and Hyundai executives to deepen chip and hardware partnerships. Google DeepMind will open an AI campus in Seoul — described as the first of its kind globally. ([UPI](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2026/04/28/Google-DeepMind-CEO-meets-South-Korean-business-leaders/2091777409196/)) ([Korea Herald](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/10726734))

- **Yann LeCun** (now at AMI Labs) told a capacity crowd at Brown University: "AI sucks... We have systems that can manipulate language, and they fool us into thinking they are smart." Called hundreds of billions invested in LLMs reaching human-level intelligence "complete BS." Separately introduced JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture) as an alternative to generative AI's scaling-focused approach. ([Brown University](https://www.brown.edu/news/2026-04-01/yann-lecun-artificial-intelligence-pioneer))

- **Andrej Karpathy** proposed using LLMs to build persistent personal knowledge bases ("LLM Wiki") rather than starting each query from scratch, processing documents at ingestion time into structured wiki-like products. Also published an essay on "AI Psychosis" — the growing perception gap between how developers experience AI and how the public does. ([X/Twitter](https://x.com/karpathy/status/2039805659525644595)) ([The New Stack](https://thenewstack.io/karpathy-says-developers-have-ai-psychosis-everyone-else-is-next/))

- **Sam Altman** announced OpenAI's expanded partnership with AWS, bringing Codex to AWS customers alongside the existing Microsoft relationship. This comes as the Musk trial puts him under intense public scrutiny. ([Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/2026/an-interview-with-openai-ceo-sam-altman-and-aws-ceo-matt-garman-about-bedrock-managed-agents/))

## Other Notable AI News

- **Google signs classified AI deal with Pentagon** (April 28) — Allows the DoD to use Gemini models for "any lawful government purpose" on classified networks. Google agreed to "assist in adjusting its AI safety settings and filters at the government's request." Over 600 Google employees signed a letter opposing the deal. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-28/google-allows-pentagon-to-use-its-ai-in-classified-military-work)) ([9to5Google](https://9to5google.com/2026/04/28/googles-updated-pentagon-deal-uses-gemini-for-any-lawful-government-purpose-with-classified-data/))

- **China blocks Meta's $2B Manus acquisition** — Beijing's National Development and Reform Commission ordered the deal unwound on national security grounds. Complication: Manus employees have already joined Meta's AI team, and investors like Tencent have received payouts. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/27/meta-manus-china-blocks-acquisition-ai-startup.html)) ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/28/china-blocks-meta-manus-deal-ai/))

- **Cognition AI (Devin) seeks $25B valuation** — The AI coding startup is in early talks to raise hundreds of millions, more than doubling its $10.2B valuation. Devin grew ARR from $1M (Sep 2024) to $73M (Jun 2025). ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-23/ai-coding-firm-cognition-in-funding-talks-at-25-billion-value))

- **EU AI Act trilogue** (April 28) — Negotiators working to close the Digital Omnibus package before the August 2, 2026 obligation for high-risk systems takes effect. HR tools with automated screening remain classified as high-risk.

- **Q1 2026 venture funding shattered records** — AI accounted for 81% of global venture funding. Four mega-deals (OpenAI $122B, Anthropic $30B, xAI $20B, Waymo $16B) totaled $188B, exceeding all 2024 global venture funding combined. ([Crunchbase](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/record-breaking-funding-ai-global-q1-2026/))

- **Enterprise AI agent sprawl** — OutSystems survey of ~1,900 IT leaders found 96% of enterprises run AI agents, but 94% are concerned about sprawl increasing complexity, debt, and security risk, with only 12% having a central management platform.
