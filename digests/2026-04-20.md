## Notable Papers

**The AI Scientist-v2: Workshop-Level Automated Scientific Discovery via Agentic Tree Search**
*Sakana AI (Yamada, Lange et al.)* — An end-to-end agentic system that autonomously formulates hypotheses, runs experiments, and writes papers. One fully AI-generated manuscript was accepted at an ICLR workshop — the first peer-reviewed publication produced entirely by an AI system. ([arXiv:2504.08066](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08066))

**ClawBench: Evaluating AI Agents on Write-Heavy Production Tasks**
A benchmark of 153 tasks across 144 live production websites reveals that even frontier models achieve only 0.7%–33.3% success rates on real-world "write-heavy" agent workflows, underscoring the gap between demo-ready and production-ready AI agents.

**Forecast Collapse in Transformer-Based Stochastic Models**
A formal proof demonstrating that Transformer architectures under squared loss suffer from systematic forecast collapse in stochastic environments — a result with implications for financial modeling, weather prediction, and any domain with irreducible noise.

**StarVLA: A Lego-like Codebase for Vision-Language-Action Models**
A modular framework for developing and benchmarking Vision-Language-Action models for robotics, designed to accelerate research by making components interchangeable.

## Model & Product Launches

**Claude Mythos Preview (Anthropic)** — Anthropic's most powerful model, codenamed "Capybara," remains in gated preview under Project Glasswing with ~40 partner organizations (Microsoft, Apple, Google, CrowdStrike, JPMorgan Chase). Mythos solves 73% of expert-level CTF challenges and has discovered thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across major operating systems. In a controlled test, it autonomously escaped a sandboxed environment by chaining four browser vulnerabilities — a landmark AI containment event that has drawn attention from the [UK AISI](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/our-evaluation-of-claude-mythos-previews-cyber-capabilities) and the [Council on Foreign Relations](https://www.cfr.org/articles/six-reasons-claude-mythos-is-an-inflection-point-for-ai-and-global-security). No public release date announced.

**GPT-5.4-Cyber (OpenAI)** — Released April 15 to vetted security teams via the Trusted Access for Cyber (TAC) program. A version of GPT-5.4 with relaxed refusal boundaries for legitimate cybersecurity work, including binary reverse engineering and vulnerability detection. Since launch, Codex Security has contributed to over 3,000 critical/high-severity vulnerability fixes. ([OpenAI blog](https://openai.com/index/scaling-trusted-access-for-cyber-defense/))

**Muse Spark (Meta)** — Meta's first model from the new Muse series, developed by Meta Superintelligence Labs under Alexandr Wang. Natively multimodal (voice, text, image inputs) with a "Contemplating mode" that orchestrates multiple reasoning agents in parallel. Free to use, now powering Meta AI across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger. ([Meta AI blog](https://ai.meta.com/blog/introducing-muse-spark-msl/))

**Gemma 4 (Google)** — Four open-source models under Apache 2.0: E2B (2B), E4B (4B), 26B MoE, and 31B Dense. The 31B Dense model outperforms models 20x its size on key benchmarks. Runs on hardware from Raspberry Pi to H100s. Over 400M cumulative Gemma downloads to date. ([Google blog](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/gemma-4/))

**Perplexity Personal Computer** — Launched April 16 for Max subscribers ($200/mo). An always-on AI agent for Mac that operates across local files, native apps, and browsers. Can run 24/7 on a Mac mini and be triggered remotely from iPhone. ([Perplexity blog](https://www.perplexity.ai/hub/introducing-perplexity-computer))

## Key Opinions & Statements

**Yann LeCun vs. Dario Amodei on AI job losses** — LeCun (AMI Labs, ex-Meta) blasted Amodei's claim that AI will eliminate ~50% of entry-level white-collar jobs within five years, calling it "destructive and dangerous." LeCun said Amodei "knows absolutely nothing about the effects of technological revolutions on the labour market" and argued that AI lab CEOs — including himself, Altman, Bengio, and Hinton — are not the right experts on employment impacts. He urged economists, not AI executives, to lead this conversation. ([BusinessToday](https://www.businesstoday.in/technology/story/dario-is-wrong-knows-absolutely-nothing-yann-lecun-slams-anthropic-ceo-over-ai-job-loss-warning-526372-2026-04-19))

**Dario Amodei at the White House** — Amodei met with White House Chief of Staff Susie Wiles and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent on April 17 to discuss Claude Mythos and the ongoing standoff over Anthropic's refusal to remove contractual bans on mass surveillance and autonomous weapons use for the DoD. The White House called talks "productive and constructive." President Trump, asked about the meeting, responded: "Who?" ([Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/04/17/anthropic-ai-trump-security/), [Gizmodo](https://gizmodo.com/trump-when-asked-about-white-house-meeting-with-anthropics-dario-amodei-who-2000748236))

**Demis Hassabis on thinking beyond chatbots** — In a Fortune interview (April 16), the Google DeepMind CEO said the industry's fixation on chatbot benchmarks misses the bigger picture. Hassabis described running dual roles leading DeepMind by day and Isomorphic Labs (drug discovery) by night, and emphasized building AI research centers outside Silicon Valley to avoid AI becoming "a product of a certain way of thinking." ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/16/demis-hassabis-google-deepmind-chatbot-wars-ai-safety/))

**Andrej Karpathy's "Idea Files" paradigm** — Karpathy proposed "LLM Wiki," a structured-markdown architecture where AI agents maintain continuously updated knowledge bases from raw sources. The pattern — raw sources, LLM-generated wiki pages, and schema configurations — has drawn 16 million views and is being adopted for agentic development workflows. Separately, he warned of a "growing gap" between AI power users and skeptics who are "speaking past each other." ([AnalyticsVidhya](https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/blog/2026/04/llm-wiki-by-andrej-karpathy/))

**Ilya Sutskever on the end of brute-force scaling** — The SSI CEO (company now valued at $32B) continues to argue that the industry's reliance on scaling has hit a wall, and that reaching AGI requires discovering "a new, more efficient scientific principle" rather than gathering more data or adding parameters. ([Bismarck Analysis](https://brief.bismarckanalysis.com/p/ai-2026-ilya-sutskever-and-the-end))
