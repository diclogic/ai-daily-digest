## Notable Papers & Research

**"Agentic AI Orchestration Should be Bayes-consistent"** — A position paper from a group of 30 researchers argues that agentic AI control layers must be grounded in Bayesian principles, contending that while making LLMs themselves Bayesian is computationally prohibitive, the orchestration layer should be Bayes-consistent. ([arXiv, May 4](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

**Deep Learning Generalization via Neural Tangent Kernel** — Stanford researchers developed a generalization theory for deep neural networks showing how the empirical NTK partitions output space into a signal channel and a test-invisible reservoir, achieving 5x speedup in grokking and improving reward accuracy in LLM fine-tuning under noisy preferences. ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.LG/current))

**RLDX-1: Unified Vision-Language-Action for Dexterous Robotics** — Developed by RLWRLD and KAIST, this system demonstrates a 97.8% success rate on the LIBERO benchmark for dexterous robotic manipulation, outperforming prior VLA approaches across both simulation and real-world tasks. ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

**MinerU2.5** — A 1.2B-parameter document parsing vision-language model achieves state-of-the-art recognition accuracy with strong computational efficiency through a coarse-to-fine parsing strategy. ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

## Key Opinions & Statements

**Dario Amodei (Anthropic)** — At Anthropic's financial services briefing in NYC on May 5, Amodei warned of a cybersecurity "moment of danger," stating that Claude Mythos has uncovered thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and browser. He said the industry has "roughly six to twelve months" to patch them before rival AI models from China catch up. Separately, he described Anthropic's 80-fold Q1 revenue surge as "too hard to handle," attributing capacity constraints to explosive demand. He also invoked the Jevons Paradox when discussing AI and jobs, shifting from his earlier warnings about white-collar displacement. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/05/anthropic-ceo-cyber-moment-of-danger-mythos-vulnerabilities.html)) ([Benzinga](https://www.benzinga.com/markets/equities/26/05/52355359/dario-amodei-anthropic-explosive-growth-too-hard-to-handle-80-fold-q1-surge))

**Yann LeCun (AMI Labs, ex-Meta)** — In an Axios interview, LeCun called predictions that AI will erase 20% of jobs "ridiculously stupid" and "extremely destructive," warning the doom narrative is pushing teens into depression. He said AI tools are "still not very good at reasoning" and emphasized that AI will amplify human intelligence, not replace it. LeCun left Meta in late 2025 to launch AMI Labs ($1.03B seed) betting that LLMs alone will never reach human-level intelligence. ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/05/ai-job-apocalypse-warnings-destructive-yann-lecun/)) ([Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/04/ai-godfather-survival-guide-hype-doom))

**Andrej Karpathy (Eureka Labs)** — At Sequoia's AI Ascent 2026, Karpathy marked the one-year anniversary of coining "vibe coding" by proposing its evolution into "agentic engineering" — where you design the system and specify constraints, then use AI to accelerate implementation. He stated: "We're still very much in the realm of pattern matching, and we need to bridge the gap towards true reasoning." ([Sequoia AI Ascent](https://www.theaiopportunities.com/p/sequoia-ai-ascent-2026-andrej-karpathy)) ([SD Times](https://sdtimes.com/ai/andrej-karpathy-has-renamed-vibe-coding-heres-what-engineering-leaders-need-to-do-about-it/))

**Mira Murati (ex-OpenAI CTO, via trial testimony)** — In video deposition shown during the Musk v. OpenAI trial, Murati testified that Sam Altman falsely told her OpenAI's legal team had cleared a new AI model to bypass an internal safety review board. She said she verified with general counsel Jason Kwon and found a "misalignment," then ensured the model underwent review anyway. Murati described Altman as "creating chaos" and being deceptive, and confirmed he "undermined" her and "pitted other execs against one another." ([Futurism](https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/perjury-openai-cto-murati-altman)) ([US News](https://www.usnews.com/news/top-news/articles/2026-05-06/in-openai-trial-former-technology-chief-says-altman-sowed-chaos-distrust-among-top-executives))

**Ilya Sutskever (Safe Superintelligence)** — Sutskever continues to advocate for new architectures beyond scaling, stating that current models are plateauing and pretraining results have flattened. He received the National Academy of Sciences Award for Industrial Application of Science in 2026, the first time the award was given for work in AI. ([Bismarck Analysis](https://brief.bismarckanalysis.com/p/ai-2026-ilya-sutskever-and-the-end))
