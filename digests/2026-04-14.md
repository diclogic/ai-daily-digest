## Notable Papers

**The AI Scientist-v2: Workshop-Level Automated Scientific Discovery via Agentic Tree Search**
*Sakana AI — arXiv:2504.08066*
A fully agentic system that autonomously proposes hypotheses, runs experiments, analyzes data, and writes manuscripts; a paper it generated was accepted at an ICLR workshop — the first AI-authored paper to clear peer review. Unlike v1, it requires no human-authored code templates and generalizes across ML domains via a novel progressive tree-search approach.
[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08066)

**PaperOrchestra: A Multi-Agent Framework for Automated AI Research Paper Writing**
*Google Cloud AI Research — arXiv:2604.05018*
Five specialized agents (Outline, Plotting, Literature Review, Section Writing, Refinement) convert raw experimental logs and an idea summary into a submission-ready LaTeX manuscript with API-verified citations. In human evaluations it beats autonomous baselines by 50–68% on literature review quality. Comes with PaperWritingBench, a new 200-paper evaluation benchmark.
[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05018)

**ClawBench: Evaluating Agents on Live Production Websites**
Introduced a 153-task "write-heavy" benchmark across 144 live websites; frontier models achieve only 0.7%–33.3% success on these realistic tasks, underscoring how far agentic AI remains from reliable real-world deployment.

**Crashing Waves vs. Rising Tides: AI Automation Patterns**
*MIT Future Tech / Labor Markets*
Proposes two models of AI-driven labor displacement: abrupt capability surges on specific tasks ("crashing waves") versus continuous broad-based improvement ("rising tides"). Evidence points to rising tides as the dominant mechanism — with employment for software developers aged 22–25 already down ~20% since 2022.
[MIT Future Tech](https://futuretech.mit.edu/publication/crashing-waves-vs-rising-tides-preliminary-findings-on-ai-automation-from-thousands-of-worker-evaluations-of-labor-market-tasks)

## Model & Product Launches

**Claude Mythos Preview — Anthropic (via Project Glasswing)**
Announced April 7; expanded to Amazon Bedrock on April 13. Mythos is Anthropic's most capable model and is focused on cybersecurity: it autonomously identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and browser, including a 17-year-old FreeBSD RCE (CVE-2026-4747). Benchmarks: 93.9% SWE-bench Verified (vs. 80.8% for Opus 4.6) and 97.6% on USAMO 2026 (vs. 42.3% for Opus 4.6). **Anthropic will not make Mythos generally available;** access is restricted to ~50 Project Glasswing partners for coordinated vulnerability disclosure. The eventual goal is "safely deploying Mythos-class models at scale."
[Anthropic](https://red.anthropic.com/2026/mythos-preview/) | [Amazon Bedrock](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/bedrock/latest/userguide/model-card-anthropic-claude-mythos-preview.html) | [InfoQ](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/04/anthropic-claude-mythos/)

**Meta Muse Spark**
Meta's first-ever proprietary (closed-weights, undisclosed architecture) model, built in nine months by Alexandr Wang's Meta Superintelligence Labs team. Scores 52 on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index v4.0 (behind GPT-5.4 at 57, Gemini 3.1 Pro at 57, and Claude Opus 4.6 at 53). Leads rivals on health benchmarks (HealthBench Hard: 42.8) and Humanity's Last Exam (50.2%), but trails notably in coding (Terminal-Bench: 59.0 vs GPT-5.4's 75.1) and ARC-AGI-2 reasoning. Marks a significant strategic reversal from Meta's open-source-first Llama approach.
[Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/08/meta-muse-alexandr-wang) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/08/meta-debuts-the-muse-spark-model-in-a-ground-up-overhaul-of-its-ai/)

**Google Gemma 4** *(April 2)*
Released under Apache 2.0. Codeforces ELO jumped from 110 (Gemma 3) to 2150 — a 20x improvement in competitive coding — while requiring far less hardware than Llama 4 Maverick.

**Meta Llama 4 (Scout & Maverick)** *(Early April)*
First Llama models with Mixture-of-Experts architecture and native multimodal training from pretraining. Scout: 17B active / 109B total parameters with a 10-million-token context window.

**Claude Code — Standalone Product**
Anthropic launched Claude Code as a standalone terminal-native AI agent capable of cloning repos, writing and running tests, fixing CI pipelines, and opening PRs. Powered by Claude Sonnet 4.6 (Opus 4.6 backend for complex tasks); integrates with GitHub, GitLab, and Jira; supports sandboxed execution.

**OpenAI: o3-mini Default + Flex Compute**
o3-mini replaced o1-mini as the default reasoning model for ChatGPT Plus (3× faster at equivalent or better quality). "Flex compute" offers o3 at a 30% discount during off-peak hours.

**Demis Hassabis vs. Yann LeCun — The AGI Definition War**
LeCun argued publicly that *"there is no such thing as general intelligence — this concept makes absolutely no sense,"* maintaining that LLMs can never achieve humanlike intelligence and that an entirely different approach is needed. Hassabis fired back on X, saying LeCun is *"confusing general intelligence with universal intelligence"* and that the human brain is demonstrably a broadly capable learning system. Hassabis put 50/50 odds on AGI within the decade. (Elon Musk sided with Hassabis.) The dispute has real stakes: it frames competing R&D philosophies at Meta, Google DeepMind, and every major lab.

## Other Notable AI News

**Stanford AI Index 2026: China Nearly Neck-and-Neck with the US**
Published April 13, the annual Stanford HAI report's headline finding: Anthropic leads the Arena model rankings as of March 2026, but Chinese labs (DeepSeek, Alibaba) trail only modestly. China now leads the US in total AI patents and model publication volume; the US still leads in top-tier models and high-impact patents. Other key data: generative AI reached 53% consumer adoption in three years (faster than PCs or the internet); organizational adoption is 88%; AI is estimated to generate $172B in annual consumer value in the US. Notably, transparency is declining — the Foundation Model Transparency Index dropped from 58 to 40 points as the largest labs increasingly withhold training details.
[Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report) | [IEEE Spectrum](https://spectrum.ieee.org/state-of-ai-index-2026) | [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/04/13/stanford-hais-2026-ai-index-reveals-china-u-s-now-neck-neck-race-global-dominance/)
