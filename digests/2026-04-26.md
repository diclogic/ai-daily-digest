## Notable Papers

- **Forecast Collapse of Transformer-Based Models Under Squared Loss** — Andreoletti (2026). A formal proof that standard Transformer architectures exhibit forecast collapse in stochastic environments under squared loss, with significant implications for financial and time-series forecasting. The result suggests fundamental architectural limitations beyond what scaling can address. [arXiv (April 2026)](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

- **QED-Nano: Teaching a Tiny Model to Prove Hard Theorems** — Demonstrates that small, distilled models can match larger systems on formal mathematical proof tasks, suggesting that reasoning capability may be more compressible than previously believed. [arXiv (April 2026)](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

- **StarVLA: A Lego-like Codebase for Vision-Language-Action Model Developing** — Introduces a modular framework for building and composing VLA models, lowering the barrier to robotics research by enabling plug-and-play combinations of vision encoders, language models, and action decoders. [arXiv (April 2026)](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

- **Collaborative AI Agents and Critics for Fault Detection** — Proposes a multi-agent architecture where specialized AI agents and critic agents collaborate to identify faults in complex systems, outperforming single-agent approaches on industrial fault-detection benchmarks. [arXiv (April 2026)](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

## Key Opinions & Statements

### Geoffrey Hinton — "Apply the Brakes"
At the Digital World Conference in Geneva, the Nobel laureate and "Godfather of AI" issued his strongest warning yet, comparing unregulated AI to "a very fast car with no steering wheel." Hinton said only about 1% of AI work goes toward safety, warned that "we don't know whether we can co-exist with super intelligent AI," and pushed back on industry framing that equates regulation with slowing progress. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/04/1167361) | [Malay Mail](https://www.malaymail.com/news/tech-gadgets/2026/04/22/like-a-car-with-no-steering-wheel-ai-pioneer-geoffrey-hinton-sounds-alarm-on-unregulated-tech/217196)

### Sam Altman — Tumbler Ridge Fallout Continues
Canada's AI minister said OpenAI's voluntary commitments "do not go far enough" following Altman's apology yesterday. A joint ISED–Public Safety task force announced today is reviewing AI safety reporting protocols, with preliminary recommendations expected by summer. The case has intensified calls for mandatory threat-reporting requirements for AI companies operating in Canada. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/25/openai-ceo-apologizes-to-tumbler-ridge-community/) | [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/sam-altman-openai-apology-tumbler-ridge-shooting)

### Yann LeCun — JEPA Framework and AI Jobs Debate
LeCun continues his campaign against LLM-centric AI, introducing an updated Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA) framework on April 23 that predicts in compressed abstract representation space rather than generating tokens. He also sharply criticized Dario Amodei's prediction that AI will eliminate 50% of entry-level jobs, calling it "destructive and dangerous" and arguing that AI lab CEOs are the wrong experts on labor market impacts. [GuruFocus](https://www.gurufocus.com/news/8814390/exploring-leworldmodel-yann-lecuns-innovative-ai-approach) | [StartupNews](https://startupnews.fyi/2026/04/19/ai-pioneer-yann-lecun-rejects-anthropics-50-percent-job-loss-prediction-destructive-and-dangerous/)

### Dario Amodei — White House and Workforce
Amodei's week-old warning that AI could push U.S. unemployment to 10–20% continues to draw criticism. Meanwhile, Anthropic's legal battle with the Trump administration over the Pentagon blacklist remains in limbo: a federal judge blocked the ban for non-DOD agencies, but the DC Circuit refused to bar DOD from cutting ties while litigation proceeds. [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/17/business/anthropic-white-house-meeting-dario-amodei) | [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/08/anthropic-pentagon-court-ruling-supply-chain-risk.html)

### Andrej Karpathy — The AI User Gap
Karpathy warned of a widening split between AI power users and skeptics, arguing that many people judged AI based on last year's free-tier ChatGPT and never updated their mental models. He says two distinct user groups are emerging: those who treat AI as a composable, programmable tool, and those who primarily judge it by high-level societal risk. [X (Karpathy)](https://x.com/karpathy/status/2042334451611693415) | [Let's Data Science](https://letsdatascience.com/news/karpathy-warns-growing-gap-among-ai-users-a6adb56d)
