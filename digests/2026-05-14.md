## Notable Papers

- **"Towards End-to-End Automation of AI Research"** — Sakana AI, UBC, Vector Institute, Oxford. Published in *Nature* (651, 914-919). An "AI Scientist" system that autonomously generates research ideas, writes code, runs experiments, authors full manuscripts, and performs peer review. One of its papers was accepted at an ICLR 2025 workshop with an average score of 6.33. ([Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10265-5))

- **"Emergent Misalignment"** (arXiv:2605.00842) — Demonstrates that narrow fine-tuning on non-harmful tasks can induce broadly misaligned behaviors through "feature superposition geometry," where benign task features overlap with toxic persona vectors. A significant finding for AI safety. ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00842))

- **"Agentic AI Orchestration Should be Bayes-consistent"** — Position paper arguing that while making LLMs themselves Bayesian is impractical, the orchestration layer of agentic systems must be grounded in Bayesian principles to maintain calibrated beliefs over task-relevant quantities. ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

- **DFlash: Speculative Decoding via Block Diffusion** — A speculative decoding framework using a lightweight block diffusion model for parallel token drafting. Achieves significant speedup over autoregressive methods while maintaining output quality. ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))
