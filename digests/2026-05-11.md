## Notable Papers

### Agentic AI Orchestration Should Be Bayes-Consistent
**Authors:** Multiple (position paper) | **Published:** May 4, 2026

Argues that the control layer of any agentic AI system must be grounded in Bayesian principles to ensure reliable decision-making. As multi-agent deployments scale rapidly, the paper contends that ad-hoc orchestration approaches will fail and proposes a formal framework for Bayes-consistent agent coordination.

[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

### UniPool: A Globally Shared Expert Pool for Mixture-of-Experts
**Published:** May 4, 2026

Proposes a globally shared expert pool architecture for MoE models, addressing the inefficiency of duplicated experts across layers. Relevant as MoE architectures dominate the latest generation of Chinese open-weight models (DeepSeek V4, Kimi K2.6).

[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

### ARIS: Cross-Model Adversarial Collaboration for Research
**Authors:** Shanghai Jiao Tong University | **Published:** May 4, 2026

An open-source research harness that uses cross-model adversarial collaboration — coordinating execution, orchestration, and assurance layers — to ensure reliable long-term research outcomes. Reflects the growing trend toward multi-agent systems for scientific discovery.

OpenAI's GPT-5.5 Instant continued its rollout to all ChatGPT users over the weekend, now serving as the default model. ## Key Opinions & Statements

### Yann LeCun — "20% Job Loss Prediction Is Ridiculously Stupid"
In a wide-ranging [Axios interview](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/04/ai-godfather-survival-guide-hype-doom) published this weekend, LeCun called CEO-driven AI job loss narratives "extremely destructive," warning they are pushing teenagers into depression and bad career decisions. He advised people to listen to economists instead, noting that historically it takes new technologies 15 years to achieve their promise in productivity gains. LeCun's AMI Labs ($1.03B seed) continues building its JEPA-based alternative to LLMs.

[Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/05/ai-job-apocalypse-warnings-destructive-yann-lecun/)

### Dario Amodei — From Job Loss Warnings to Jevons Paradox
In a notable rhetorical shift, Amodei is now publicly entertaining [Jevons Paradox](https://fortune.com/2026/05/05/dario-amodei-jevons-paradox-will-ai-wipe-out-white-collar-jobs/) — the idea that AI-driven efficiency could create more demand and ultimately more jobs, not fewer. This is a marked change from his previous warnings about imminent white-collar displacement, and aligns with LeCun's pushback on doom narratives.

### Andrej Karpathy — "Understanding Becomes the Bottleneck"
Karpathy's AI Ascent 2026 talk continues generating discussion. His key insight: as AI gets better, the temptation is to learn less, but understanding becomes the bottleneck. "We're still very much in the realm of pattern matching, and we need to bridge the gap towards true reasoning." His framework — vibe coding for accessibility, agentic engineering for quality — has become a widely referenced taxonomy.

[Sequoia AI Ascent summary](https://karpathy.bearblog.dev/sequoia-ascent-2026/)

## Other Notable AI News

### Nvidia Surpasses $40B in AI Equity Investments
Nvidia committed over $40 billion in equity deals in 2026, led by a $30 billion stake in OpenAI. This week alone: a $2.1B investment right in data center operator IREN (plus a $3.4B five-year managed GPU cloud deal) and up to $3.2B in Corning, which will build three new U.S. optical manufacturing facilities and create 3,000+ jobs. Critics note concerns about circular deals — Nvidia is investing in its own customers.

[CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/09/nvidia-embraces-ai-investor-topping-40-billion-in-equity-bets-2026.html) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/09/nvidia-has-already-committed-40b-to-equity-ai-deals-this-year/)

### Utah AI Data Center Sparks Protests
Box Elder County commissioners approved a 40,000-acre AI data center campus ("Stratos") backed by Kevin O'Leary in rural Utah near the Great Salt Lake, despite fierce community opposition. The project would more than double Utah's electricity usage, increase its carbon footprint by 50%, and draw water from a tributary feeding the already-depleted Great Salt Lake. Nearly 4,000 Utahns filed formal protests. O'Leary dismissed critics as "paid activists."

[CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/09/tech/ai-data-center-utah-kevin-oleary-opposition) | [Gizmodo](https://gizmodo.com/kevin-olearys-massive-data-center-project-in-utah-gets-the-greenlight-locals-are-furious-2000755168)

### CNN: AI Is Reshaping Jobs, Not Replacing Them
A [CNN analysis](https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/10/tech/ai-taking-jobs) published yesterday argues that while AI was the top cited reason for job cuts in April, the reality is more nuanced — companies are automating parts of jobs rather than eliminating positions wholesale. Software engineering is cited as the paradigm: execution now involves a mix of writing code and prompting AI, but the role itself persists.

### JPMorgan Reclassifies AI as Core Infrastructure
JPMorgan Chase formally moved its AI investments from experimental R&D to core infrastructure, with a 2026 technology budget of ~$19.8B, 2,000 AI-dedicated staff, and AI projected to generate $2.5B in annual value. This follows the bank's high-profile partnership with Anthropic announced last week.
