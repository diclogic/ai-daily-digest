## Notable Papers

- **Embedded Language Flows (ELF)** — Establishes a continuous diffusion language model paradigm using Flow Matching, achieving competitive generative quality in translation and summarization while requiring ~10x fewer training tokens. ([arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

- **World Action Models (WAMs)** — An emerging paradigm in embodied AI that unifies predictive physical understanding with action generation, enabling agents to anticipate environment changes under their interventions. Provides a formal definition and structured architectural taxonomy. ([arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

- **Attractor Models** — Proposes an architecture that frames latent refinement as a fixed-point problem, enabling stable and memory-efficient iterative computation for complex reasoning tasks in language models. ([arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

- **EffOPD (Efficient On-Policy Distillation)** — Comprehensive empirical study on On-Policy Distillation in LLMs, demonstrating that "functional redundancy avoidance" and "early low-rank lock-in" enable 3x training acceleration while maintaining or improving final performance. ([arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

- **MinerU2.5** — A 1.2B-parameter document parsing vision-language model achieving state-of-the-art recognition accuracy with computational efficiency through a coarse-to-fine parsing strategy. ([arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

- **SenseNova-U1** — Presents a native unified multimodal paradigm via NEO-unify architecture that directly processes pixels and words, achieving 0.940 average word accuracy on CVTG-2K for text rendering. ([arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

## Model & Product Launches

### Claude Mythos Preview (Anthropic) — Restricted Release
Anthropic's next-generation frontier model, announced April 7, remains in gated preview via **Project Glasswing** due to its extraordinary cybersecurity capabilities. Mythos identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and browser — including a 27-year-old flaw in OpenBSD — and developed working exploits on first attempt in 83%+ of cases. In one demonstration, it chained four independent bugs to bypass both browser renderer and OS sandboxing. Project Glasswing partners include AWS, Apple, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorgan Chase, Microsoft, NVIDIA, and Palo Alto Networks. General public release is indefinitely deferred due to security concerns. ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/glasswing)) ([Foreign Policy](https://foreignpolicy.com/2026/04/20/claude-mythos-preview-anthropic-project-glasswing-cybersecurity-ai-hacking-danger/))

### GPT-5.5 Instant (OpenAI) — Now ChatGPT Default
Released May 5 as the new default model for all ChatGPT users, replacing GPT-5.3 Instant. Key improvements: 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims on high-stakes prompts (medicine, law, finance), 30% less verbose output, and enhanced personalization from past chats for Plus/Pro users. GPT-5.3 Instant remains available for paid users for three months. ([OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-instant/)) ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/05/openai-releases-gpt-5-5-instant-a-new-default-model-for-chatgpt/))

### Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite (Google) — Generally Available
Google's most cost-efficient Gemini 3 model went GA on May 8. Priced at $0.25/1M input tokens, it matches Gemini 2.5 Flash performance while offering 2.5x faster time-to-first-token and 45% faster output speed. Supports configurable thinking levels (minimal to high). ([Google Cloud Blog](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/gemini-3-1-flash-lite-is-now-generally-available))

### SubQ (Subquadratic) — Early Access
Miami-based startup Subquadratic emerged from stealth on May 5 with $29M in seed funding and SubQ, the first commercial subquadratic LLM featuring a 12-million-token context window. Built on Subquadratic Sparse Attention (SSA), it claims ~1,000x compute reduction at full context length. On the RULER 128K benchmark: 95% accuracy at $8 vs. 94% at ~$2,600 for comparable models. Currently waitlist-only. ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/05/subquadratic-launches-29m-bring-12m-token-context-windows-ai/))

### ZAYA1-8B (Zyphra) — Open Source
An open-source MoE reasoning model with 8B total parameters (~760M active per token), trained end-to-end on AMD Instinct hardware. Notable for its efficiency-focused architecture.

## Key Opinions & Statements

At a May 5 event alongside JPMorgan Chase CEO Jamie Dimon, Amodei warned that AI has created a narrow "moment of danger" for the world's software infrastructure, citing thousands of vulnerabilities discovered by Claude Mythos. He called Anthropic's 80x quarterly growth "too hard to handle" and described the company's decision to rent capacity at Elon Musk's Colossus 1 data center in Memphis as a necessity driven by explosive demand. Separately, Amodei has pivoted his public messaging on AI jobs, invoking Jevons Paradox to argue that AI-driven productivity gains will create more demand for human workers, not less. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/video/2026/05/05/anthropic-ceo-dario-amodei-speaks-with-jamie-dimon-here-are-key-takeaways.html)) ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/05/dario-amodei-jevons-paradox-will-ai-wipe-out-white-collar-jobs/))

Separately, reports emerged that Altman has discussed starting a new AI compute company, majority-owned by OpenAI but independently fundraised. ## Other Notable AI News

### Musk v. Altman Trial Heads to Jury
Closing arguments concluded May 14 in the landmark case. Musk seeks to revert OpenAI to nonprofit status, remove Altman and Brockman from the board, and redirect $130B+ to the nonprofit arm. The jury's verdict will be advisory; Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers makes the final ruling, expected next week. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/14/closing-arguments-jury-openai-musk-altman.html))

### Anthropic Surpasses OpenAI in Revenue
Anthropic hit a $30B annualized run rate (up from $9B at end of 2025), overtaking OpenAI's $24B. Growth is driven by enterprise adoption of agentic workflows, with Claude Code alone generating $2.5B+ in annualized revenue. The company is approaching a ~$1 trillion valuation on secondary markets. ([VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-says-it-hit-a-30-billion-revenue-run-rate-after-crazy-80x-growth/))

### Recursive Superintelligence Exits Stealth ($650M Raise)
London-based startup emerged on May 13 with $650M at a $4.65B valuation, backed by GV (Google Ventures), Greycroft, NVIDIA, and AMD. Co-founded by Richard Socher (ex-Salesforce chief scientist) and Tim Rocktaschel (UCL professor, ex-DeepMind), the company is building AI that recursively improves itself. A "Level 1" autonomous training system is planned for mid-2026. ([Tech.eu](https://tech.eu/2026/05/13/recursive-superintelligence-emerges-from-stealth-with-650m-raise/)) ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/14/what-happens-when-ai-starts-building-itself/))

### Congressional Scrutiny of OpenAI

([Hoodline](https://hoodline.com/2026/05/comer-puts-sam-altman-on-the-hot-seat-over-openai-money-trail/))

AI accounted for $242B — 80% of total global venture funding in Q1 2026. The four largest rounds ever: OpenAI ($122B), Anthropic ($30B), xAI ($20B), and Waymo ($16B). ([Crunchbase](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/record-breaking-funding-ai-global-q1-2026/))

OpenAI is adding Codex access to the ChatGPT mobile app on iPhone, iPad, and Android, alongside new custom audience targeting for ChatGPT ads.

### JPMorgan Reclassifies AI as Core Infrastructure
2026 technology budget set at ~$19.8B with AI projected to generate $2.5B in annual value, focusing on AI agents, cybersecurity, and retail banking personalization.
