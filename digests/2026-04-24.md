## Notable Papers & Conferences

**ICLR 2026 Outstanding Papers (announced April 23)**
The conference runs April 24–28 in Rio de Janeiro. Two Outstanding Papers were named:

- **"Transformers are Inherently Succinct"** — Pascal Bergsträßer, Ryan Cotterell, Anthony Widjaja Lin. Provides a theoretical explanation for the power of the Transformer architecture by showing how succinctly it encodes certain concepts compared to alternative models like RNNs.
- **Multi-Turn LLM Evaluation** (title truncated in announcement). Demonstrates a marked decrease in LLM aptitude and reliability in multi-turn interactions with underspecified instructions — a setting that more closely reflects real-world usage. [Source](https://blog.iclr.cc/2026/04/23/announcing-the-iclr-2026-outstanding-papers/)

**The AI Scientist-v2: Workshop-Level Automated Scientific Discovery via Agentic Tree Search** — Sakana AI. An end-to-end agentic system that autonomously formulates hypotheses, runs experiments, and writes papers. Achieved the first fully AI-generated paper to pass peer review at an ICLR workshop. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08066)

**MedGemma 1.5 Technical Report** — Google DeepMind released an updated technical report for its medical AI model family. [Source](https://huggingface.co/papers/trending)

**QED-Nano: Teaching a Tiny Model to Prove Hard Theorems** — A compact model achieving strong performance on formal mathematical reasoning. [Source](https://huggingface.co/papers/trending)

## Model & Product Launches

**OpenAI GPT-5.5 (April 23)** — OpenAI's first fully retrained base model since GPT-4.5. Scores 82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 and 84.9% on GDPval. Benchmarks at 96.4% on MMLU with a million-token context window and 40% faster inference than GPT-4o. Strengths are in agentic coding, computer use, and knowledge work. Rolling out to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise users. API pricing: $5/M input, $30/M output tokens (2x GPT-5.4). Sam Altman called ChatGPT 5.5 "the last major milestone before AGI." [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/) · [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/23/openai-chatgpt-gpt-5-5-ai-model-superapp/)

**DeepSeek V4 Preview** — A 1-trillion-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model (37B active parameters at inference) with 1M-token context. Two variants released: V4-Pro (1.6T params, 49B active) and V4-Flash (284B params, 13B active). Uses hybrid Compressed Sparse Attention for 35x processing speedup and 40% energy reduction vs. V3.2. Notably runs on Huawei Ascend 950PR chips. Trained for an estimated $5.2M. [SitePoint](https://www.sitepoint.com/deepseek-v4-released-whats-new-in-the-latest-model-2026/) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro)

**Google TPU 8t & 8i (Google Cloud Next, April 22)** — Google unveiled two eighth-generation TPU variants. TPU 8t (training): 2.8x better price/performance; single superpod scales to 9,600 chips with 2PB shared HBM. TPU 8i (inference): 80% better perf-per-dollar, 288GB HBM with 384MB on-chip SRAM (3x previous gen). Both co-designed with Gemini models. [Google Blog](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/infrastructure-and-cloud/google-cloud/eighth-generation-tpu-agentic-era/)

**Claude Mythos Watch:** No new public release. Mythos Preview remains restricted to ~50 partner organizations via Project Glasswing. The model — Anthropic's most capable, with SWE-bench 93.9% and USAMO 97.6% — continues to dominate headlines due to the unauthorized access incident (see below). [Anthropic Red Team](https://red.anthropic.com/2026/mythos-preview/)

## Other Notable AI News

**Anthropic Mythos Breach Investigation** — A group of unauthorized users accessed Claude Mythos Preview through a third-party vendor environment on the day of its announcement. The group, operating via a private Discord channel, guessed the model's URL based on Anthropic's naming conventions, aided by a third-party contractor. Anthropic says it has found no evidence of access beyond the vendor environment.
