## Notable Papers & Research

**AutoResearchBench: Benchmarking Autonomous Scientific Literature Discovery**
A new benchmark evaluating AI agents' ability to conduct deep, wide-ranging research tasks. Even powerful LLMs achieved low accuracy rates, highlighting the gap between current agents and genuine autonomous research capability.
[arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

**Human Scientists Trounce the Best AI Agents on Complex Tasks**
*Nature* — A study finding that human scientists still significantly outperform the best AI agents on complex, multi-step scientific tasks, tempering expectations about near-term AI-driven research automation.
[Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01199-z)

**"This AI Knew the Answers But Didn't Understand the Questions"**
*ScienceDaily (April 29)* — Research on the Centaur AI model revealed that language comprehension remains a critical limitation — the model struggled to recognize and respond to the intent behind questions, even when it could produce correct outputs.
[ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/04/260429102035.htm)

**AutoFigure-Edit**
An end-to-end system that generates editable scientific illustrations from text descriptions and reference images, supporting flexible style adaptation and efficient refinement. Represents growing interest in AI tools for scientific communication.

## Model & Product Launches

**Claude Security — Public Beta (Anthropic, April 30)**
Anthropic launched Claude Security in public beta, an AI-powered vulnerability scanner that reads source code across files, traces data flows, examines module interactions, and reasons through logic flaws. Powered by Opus 4.7, it has been in research preview since February, where hundreds of organizations used it to find and fix exploits — including vulnerabilities existing tools had missed for years. Now available for Claude Enterprise customers.
[Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-code-security)

**Mistral Medium 3.5 (Mistral AI, April 29)**
A 128B dense open-weight model with 256k context window, achieving 77.6% on SWE-Bench Verified. Self-hostable on as few as four GPUs. Released alongside "Vibe" remote agents for asynchronous cloud coding sessions and a new Work mode in Le Chat for multi-step tasks. Priced at $1.50/$7.50 per million input/output tokens.
[Mistral Docs](https://docs.mistral.ai/models/model-cards/mistral-medium-3-5-26-04)

**Claude Mythos Preview — Update**
Anthropic's most advanced model continues its rollout through Project Glasswing, the tightly controlled cybersecurity consortium including AWS, Apple, Google, Microsoft, CrowdStrike, and others. Recent reports confirm Mythos solved 30% of "human-unsolvable" bioinformatics tasks on BioMysteryBench, and autonomously discovered a 17-year-old RCE vulnerability in FreeBSD. Anthropic has committed $100M in model usage credits to Project Glasswing. No general availability planned yet.
[Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/glasswing) | [UK AISI Evaluation](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/our-evaluation-of-claude-mythos-previews-cyber-capabilities)

**Tencent Hy-MT1.5 (Open Source)**
A remarkably compact 440MB translation model supporting 33 languages that matches commercial translation APIs and performs on par with 235B-scale models on standard benchmarks.

**Baidu ERNIE 5.1 Preview**
Ranked #13 globally and #1 among Chinese labs on LMArena's Text Arena.

**Alibaba FlashQLA**
High-performance linear attention kernels with 2-3x forward speedup and 2x backward speedup, purpose-built for agentic AI on personal devices.

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth called Amodei an "ideological lunatic" on April 30, escalating the months-long standoff over military AI use. **Andrej Karpathy (Eureka Labs)**
At Sequoia's AI Ascent 2026, Karpathy introduced "Agentic Engineering" as a framework, arguing that as AI improves the temptation is to learn less, but understanding becomes the bottleneck. His key insight: traditional software automates what you can specify; AI automates what you can verify. Developers must master enough depth to direct, inspect, and reject AI outputs.

OpenAI disclosed that a compromised third-party JavaScript library (Axios v1.14.1) was used in a supply chain attack affecting its macOS app-signing workflow on March 31. Though no user data was accessed, OpenAI revoked and rotated its code-signing certificate. All macOS users must update before May 8 or their apps will be blocked.
