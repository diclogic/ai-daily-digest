## Notable Papers

- **"Position: Agentic AI Orchestration Should Be Bayes-Consistent"** — A position paper by 30 cross-industry researchers argues that while making LLMs themselves Bayesian is computationally prohibitive, the orchestration layer that decides which tools to call and how to allocate resources must be Bayes-consistent. Significant for the emerging agentic AI design space. ([arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

- **"MIRA: A Score for Conditional Distribution Accuracy and Model Comparison"** — By Sammy Sharief et al. Accepted as a **Spotlight Paper at ICML 2026**. Proposes a new metric for evaluating how well models capture conditional distributions. ([arXiv stat.ML](https://arxiv.org/list/stat.ML/current))

- **"Online Self-Calibration Against Hallucination in Vision-Language Models"** — Accepted at **IJCAI 2026**. Addresses the persistent hallucination problem in VLMs with an online calibration approach. ([arXiv cs.CV](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.CV/current))

- **"Topology-Preserving Neural Operator Learning via Hodge Decomposition"** — By Dongzhe Zheng, Tao Zhong, and Christine Allen-Blanchette. Accepted at **ICML 2026**. Applies Hodge decomposition to ensure neural operators preserve topological structure.

### SubQ 1M-Preview (May 5)
Miami-based startup Subquadratic emerged from stealth with $29M in seed funding and the first commercial subquadratic LLM. SubQ boasts a 12M-token context window and claims ~52x speed improvement over FlashAttention at 1M tokens, with ~1,000x compute reduction at full context. Uses a novel "Subquadratic Sparse Attention" (SSA) mechanism instead of standard transformer attention. Performance claims are eye-catching but await independent verification. ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/05/subquadratic-launches-29m-bring-12m-token-context-windows-ai/))

### Claude Mythos Update
Claude Mythos Preview remains restricted to **Project Glasswing** partners (12 founding organizations, ~40 vetted critical-infrastructure operators). No public API access. A May 17 report revealed that unauthorized users accessed Mythos on day one via credentials obtained through a third-party contractor breach combined with Mercor recruitment-platform data. Anthropic confirmed it is investigating but found no evidence of activity beyond the vendor environment. The model continues to be the subject of intense discussion for its cybersecurity capabilities, including discovery of a 27-year-old OpenBSD vulnerability. ([Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/cyber-security/how-a-cavalcade-of-blunders-gave-unauthorized-users-access-to-claude-mythos-restricted-model-accessed-by-third-parties-thanks-to-knowledge-from-data-breach), [Security Magazine](https://www.securitymagazine.com/articles/102253-security-leaders-discuss-the-claude-mythos-breach))

## Key Opinions & Statements

### Dario Amodei — "Software will become essentially free"
At the World Economic Forum in Davos on May 18, the Anthropic CEO predicted that software is approaching a price floor of zero. He illustrated this with a hypothetical of an app built for a single meeting, costing cents, used once, then discarded. This comes as Anthropic's valuation approaches $900B with an annual revenue run rate above $40B, and as the company reported 80x year-over-year Q1 revenue growth. ([The News](https://www.thenews.com.pk/latest/1402953-software-will-become-essentially-free-warns-anthropic-ceo-amodei))

### Mustafa Suleyman — 18 months until full white-collar automation
Microsoft's AI chief predicted on May 16 that AI will reach human-level performance across most professional tasks within 18 months (late 2027), specifically naming accounting, legal, marketing, and project management as most vulnerable. This generated significant pushback, with reports noting that 80% of white-collar workers are refusing AI adoption mandates. ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/13/when-will-ai-kill-white-collar-office-jobs-18-months-microsoft-mustafa-suleyman/))

### Yann LeCun — AI doom narratives are "extremely destructive"
Now leading **AMI Labs** (his $1B-funded post-Meta venture building "world models"), LeCun argued in early May that doom narratives about AI job loss are wrong and actively harmful, noting that some high school students are depressed because they've been told AI will destroy all jobs and cause human extinction. He maintains AI is "just another set of tools" and that productivity gains from new technologies historically take 15 years to materialize. ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/05/ai-godfather-survival-guide-hype-doom/))

### Andrej Karpathy — "I've never felt more behind as a programmer"
At Sequoia Capital's AI Ascent 2026, Karpathy expanded on his vibe-coding vs. agentic-engineering framework. Key insight: vibe coding raises the floor (anyone can build software) while agentic engineering raises the ceiling (professional quality at unprecedented scale). He likens current LLMs to "ghosts" with uneven capabilities and says agents are currently like interns — developers still own aesthetics, judgment, and oversight. ([Sequoia AI Ascent](https://karpathy.bearblog.dev/sequoia-ascent-2026/))

### Demis Hassabis — AGI "likely in 3-4 years"
During a Korea visit marking the AlphaGo anniversary, the Google DeepMind CEO reiterated his AGI timeline and met with President Lee Jae-myung to expand the DeepMind-Korea partnership on AI safety, student internships, and a new AI Campus in Seoul. He continues to position scientific discovery as the strongest use case for advanced AI. ([EdTech Innovation Hub](https://www.edtechinnovationhub.com/news/demis-hassabis-marks-alphago-anniversary-with-renewed-google-deepmind-push-in-korea))
