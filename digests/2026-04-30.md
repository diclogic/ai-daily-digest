## Notable Papers & Research

**The AI Scientist-v2: Workshop-Level Automated Scientific Discovery via Agentic Tree Search**
*Sakana AI (Yamada, Lange et al.)* — An end-to-end agentic system that autonomously formulates hypotheses, runs experiments, and writes papers. One fully AI-generated manuscript passed peer review at an ICLR workshop — the first time this has happened — and the work was published in *Nature*.
[arXiv:2504.08066](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08066)

**MedGemma 1.5 Technical Report**
*Google Research* — Updated open medical AI model family (4B multimodal, 27B text, 27B multimodal) with significant gains in medical reasoning (+5% MedQA, +22% EHRQA) and new support for CT, MRI, and whole-slide histopathology imaging. Google is running a $100K MedGemma Impact Challenge on Kaggle.
[Google Developers](https://developers.google.com/health-ai-developer-foundations/medgemma)

**QED-Nano: Teaching a Tiny Model to Prove Hard Theorems**
A paper exploring how small models can be trained to handle formal mathematical proofs, part of a broader trend in compact, capable reasoning models.

**Uni-SafeBench: A Safety Benchmark for Unified Multimodal Large Models**
New benchmark for evaluating safety across multimodal LLMs, addressing a gap as models become increasingly multi-modal.

## Model & Product Launches

**GPT-5.5 "Spud" (OpenAI) — Released April 23**
OpenAI's latest flagship reclaimed #1 on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index. Key benchmarks: Terminal-Bench 2.0 at 82.7% (vs. Opus 4.7's 69.4%), FrontierMath Tier 4 at 35.4% (vs. Opus 4.7's 22.9%), and OSWorld-Verified at 78.7%. Available via API and ChatGPT.
[OpenAI announcement](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/)

**DeepSeek V4 Flash & V4 Pro (DeepSeek) — Previewed April 24**
China's DeepSeek dropped open-source V4 models that it says "close the gap" with frontier models. V4 Flash: 284B params / 13B active, priced at $0.14/M input tokens. V4 Pro: 1.6T params / 49B active, priced at $0.145/M input. Both released under open-source license.
[TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/24/deepseek-previews-new-ai-model-that-closes-the-gap-with-frontier-models/) · [MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/04/24/1136422/why-deepseeks-v4-matters/)

**Laguna XS.2 (Poolside) — Released April 28**
Poolside's first open-weight model: a 33B-param MoE (3B active) designed for agentic coding that runs on a single GPU. Scores 68.2% on SWE-bench Verified. Apache 2.0 license. Designed to run locally on a Mac with 36 GB RAM.
[Poolside blog](https://poolside.ai/blog/introducing-laguna-xs2-m1) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/poolside/Laguna-XS.2)

**OpenAI on AWS**
OpenAI expanded its partnership with AWS, bringing its models, Codex, and Amazon Bedrock Managed Agents powered by OpenAI to AWS in limited preview. Sam Altman made a virtual appearance at an AWS event on April 28.
[Stratechery interview](https://stratechery.com/2026/an-interview-with-openai-ceo-sam-altman-and-aws-ceo-matt-garman-about-bedrock-managed-agents/)

### Claude Mythos Watch

Claude Mythos Preview remains in restricted access under Project Glasswing. The model has identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and browser, including a 17-year-old FreeBSD RCE (CVE-2026-4747) and a 27-year-old OpenBSD bug. Anthropic committed $100M in usage credits and $4M in direct donations to open-source security. Mythos scores 93.9% on SWE-bench Verified (vs. Opus 4.6's 80.8%) and 97.6% on USAMO 2026. No public release is planned; access is limited to ~40 organizations in the Glasswing consortium.
[Anthropic Glasswing](https://www.anthropic.com/glasswing) · [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/04/anthropics-claude-mythos-finds.html) · [Foreign Policy](https://foreignpolicy.com/2026/04/20/claude-mythos-preview-anthropic-project-glasswing-cybersecurity-ai-hacking-danger/)

## Key Opinions & Statements

**Elon Musk** — Testified for a second day (April 29) in his trial against OpenAI in Oakland. Musk called himself "a fool" for funding OpenAI and accused its leaders of "looting the nonprofit." Under cross-examination, OpenAI attorney William Savitt pressed Musk on the fact that he pledged $1B but only contributed $38M. Musk pushed back, saying his reputation and other intangibles exceeded $100M in value. The trial is expected to last four weeks.
[NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/29/nx-s1-5803833/elon-musk-testimony-openai-altman-day-2) · [CNBC Day 3](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/29/musk-altman-live-updates-day-3-open-ai-trial.html)

**Demis Hassabis** — Visited Seoul on April 28-29 for "Google for Korea 2026," where he reunited with Go legend Lee Sedol ten years after their historic AlphaGo match. Hassabis signed an MOU with South Korea's Ministry of Science and ICT on AI research in life sciences, weather/climate, and AI safety. He announced the world's first Google AI Campus will open in Seoul's Gangnam district this year. Hassabis described AGI as opening "a new renaissance in which humanity prospers."
[UPI](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2026/04/29/korea-DeepMind-Lee-Sedol-AlphaGo-AI-meet-Seoul-Demis-Hassabis/1841777456376/) · [Korea Herald](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/10726635)

**Yann LeCun** — Criticized Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei's prediction that AI will eliminate 50% of tech jobs, calling it "destructive and dangerous." LeCun said AI lab CEOs are not the right experts on economic impacts and that economists should lead that discussion. He also dismissed concerns about Claude Mythos as "exaggerated and unnecessary drama." His startup AMI Labs ($1.03B seed, $3.5B pre-money) continues work on JEPA-based world models as a contrarian bet against LLMs.
[Startup News](https://startupnews.fyi/2026/04/19/ai-pioneer-yann-lecun-rejects-anthropics-50-percent-job-loss-prediction-destructive-and-dangerous/) · [Storyboard18](https://www.storyboard18.com/brand-makers/meta-ai-chief-yann-lecun-downplays-fears-over-anthropics-claude-mythos-94932.htm)

**Dario Amodei** — In an April interview with the Financial Times, the Anthropic CEO spoke about rapid AI progress, competitive pressure, and the lines his company refuses to cross — specifically opposing domestic mass surveillance and fully autonomous weapons in democracies. His White House meeting on April 17 about Mythos was described as "productive" by officials, though Anthropic continues to battle the Trump administration in court over the blacklisting of Claude.
[CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/17/anthropic-dario-amodei-trump-mythos.html) · [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/17/business/anthropic-white-house-meeting-dario-amodei)

**Sam Altman** — Appeared at the Oakland courthouse for the Musk trial. Separately, reports emerged of internal tension at OpenAI: CFO Sarah Friar is reportedly at odds with Altman over missed revenue targets and wants more spending discipline on data center buildout.
[Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/28/openai-cfo-sam-altman-missed-revenue-target/)

**Andrej Karpathy** — Continued advocating for AI-as-thinking-tool over AI-as-code-generator. His "autoresearch" project envisions humans as high-level research managers while AI handles execution. His "LLM Wiki" concept — dumping raw materials into a folder and having an LLM build an interlinked knowledge base — has spawned a wave of community implementations.
[Medium](https://medium.com/neuralnotions/andrej-karpathy-stopped-using-ai-to-write-code-hes-using-it-to-build-a-second-brain-instead-cddceadc5df5) · [n1n.ai](https://explore.n1n.ai/blog/autonomous-ai-research-karpathy-autoresearch-2026-04-26)
