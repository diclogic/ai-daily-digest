## Notable Papers & Research

- **"SkillsVote: Governance for Long-Horizon LLM Agents"** — Memtensor Research Group (May 18, 2026). Proposes a governance framework that manages reusable skills for LLM agents through structured collection, recommendation, and evolution processes — addressing the growing challenge of coordinating autonomous agent behavior over extended task horizons. ([arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

- **"dots.ocr: Unified Document Parsing via Vision-Language Models"** — Achieves state-of-the-art performance on document layout parsing by jointly learning layout detection, text recognition, and relational understanding. Validated on OmniDocBench and XDocParse benchmarks. ([Hugging Face Trending Papers](https://huggingface.co/papers/trending))

- **MiniCPM-o 4.5** — Enables real-time full-duplex multimodal interaction through Omni-Flow, a unified streaming framework that handles audio, video, and text simultaneously. ([Hugging Face Trending Papers](https://huggingface.co/papers/trending))

- **MinerU2.5** — A 1.2B-parameter document parsing VLM that achieves state-of-the-art recognition accuracy through a coarse-to-fine parsing strategy, offering a lightweight alternative to much larger models for structured document understanding. ([Hugging Face Trending Papers](https://huggingface.co/papers/trending))

- **AstraFlow (Carnegie Mellon)** — A dataflow-oriented reinforcement learning system enabling efficient multi-policy collaborative training and elastic scaling for large language model agents. Published May 15, 2026. ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent))

## Model & Product Launches

### Google I/O 2026 — Gemini Spark & Gemini 3.5 (May 19)
Google's biggest reveal was **Gemini Spark**, a 24/7 cloud-based agentic AI assistant powered by **Gemini 3.5** and the Google Antigravity harness. Unlike traditional assistants that respond to queries, Spark runs persistently on dedicated Google Cloud VMs, managing tasks across apps around the clock. It integrates natively with Gmail, Docs, and Slides, and connects to third-party services via MCP (Canva, OpenTable, Instacart). Example use cases include automatically parsing credit card statements for hidden fees and monitoring school inbox updates. Spark is rolling out to trusted testers this week, with beta access for Google AI Ultra subscribers ($100/month) in the U.S. next week. Google also announced **intelligent eyewear** devices launching this fall, with at least three smart glasses models from partners. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/google-introduces-gemini-spark-a-24-7-agentic-assistant-with-gmail-integration/) | [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/19/google-ai-ultra-gemini-spark-omni.html) | [Tom's Guide](https://www.tomsguide.com/news/live/google-io-2026-live-news-updates))

### OpenAI × Dell: Codex Goes On-Premises (May 18)
OpenAI and Dell Technologies announced a partnership to deploy Codex in hybrid and on-premises enterprise environments. Codex will connect with the Dell AI Data Platform and Dell AI Factory, enabling enterprises in regulated industries (finance, healthcare, government) to use Codex-powered agents without sending data to the public cloud. Codex now has over 4 million weekly active developers. This is OpenAI's first explicit hybrid/on-prem enterprise distribution play. ([OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/dell-codex-enterprise-partnership/) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/openai-co-founder-andrej-karpathy-joins-anthropics-pre-training-team/))

### Claude Mythos Preview — Watchlist Update
Claude Mythos remains in restricted access under **Project Glasswing**, serving ~50 vetted defensive cybersecurity organizations backed by a $100M Anthropic-funded credit pool. The coalition now includes AWS, Apple, Microsoft, Google, CrowdStrike, and Palo Alto Networks. Mythos has identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and browser, including a 27-year-old OpenBSD bug, a 16-year-old FFmpeg flaw, and a 17-year-old FreeBSD RCE. The model achieves ~90x improvement over Opus 4.6 at exploit development. Anthropic reaffirms Mythos is "not planned to be made generally available." Notably, Mythos's capabilities have prompted the Trump Administration to reconsider AI oversight policies it previously rejected. ([Anthropic - Project Glasswing](https://www.anthropic.com/glasswing) | [Foreign Policy](https://foreignpolicy.com/2026/04/20/claude-mythos-preview-anthropic-project-glasswing-cybersecurity-ai-hacking-danger/) | [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/06/trump-administration-embraces-ai-oversight-policies-it-once-rejected-anthropic-mythos-caisi/))

## Key Opinions & Statements

### Andrej Karpathy Joins Anthropic (May 19)
In the day's most surprising talent move, **Andrej Karpathy** — OpenAI co-founder, former Tesla AI lead, and founder of Eureka Labs — announced he has joined **Anthropic's pre-training team** under team lead Nick Joseph. He will build a new team focused on using Claude itself to accelerate pretraining research. In his announcement on X: *"I think the next few years at the frontier of LLMs will be especially formative. I am very excited to join the team here and get back to R&D."* He added that he *"remains deeply passionate about education"* and plans to resume that work in time. The hire signals Anthropic's bet that AI-assisted research — not just raw compute — is how it stays competitive. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/openai-co-founder-andrej-karpathy-joins-anthropics-pre-training-team/) | [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/19/anthropic-openai-karpathy-andrej-claude) | [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/artificial-intelligence-2/2026/andrej-karpathy-lands-at-anthropic-amid-ai-research-arms-race/))

### Elon Musk Vows Appeal After Jury Rejects OpenAI Lawsuit (May 18–19)
A nine-member jury in Oakland ruled against Musk, finding his claims were filed outside the statute of limitations — without reaching the merits of his "breach of charitable trust" allegations. The verdict came after less than two hours of deliberation. Musk called it a *"calendar technicality"* and said he will appeal to the Ninth Circuit, adding: *"She just handed out a free license to loot charities if you can keep the looting quiet for a few years!"* **Sam Altman** had testified on May 12 that *"Mr. Musk did try to kill it"* — referencing Musk's founding of xAI, talent poaching attempts, and alleged business interference. ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/18/nx-s1-5822366/musk-altman-openai-jury-verdict-claims-dismissed) | [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/05/18/jury-rejects-elon-musk-lawsuit-against-openai-its-ceo-sam-altman/) | [MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/05/18/1137488/elon-musk-suit-openai-verdict/))

### SubQ: First Non-Transformer Frontier Model Launches
Subquadratic launched **SubQ** on May 5 with $29M in seed funding, claiming the first fully sub-quadratic commercial LLM with a **12-million-token context window** at 1/5 the cost of frontier models. The architecture (Subquadratic Selective Attention) reportedly runs 52x faster than FlashAttention at 1M tokens. However, significant skepticism remains — no technical report or open weights have been released, and prior subquadratic architectures have consistently underperformed transformers at scale. ([eWeek](https://www.eweek.com/news/subquadratic-subq-12m-token-llm-neuron/) | [DataCamp](https://www.datacamp.com/blog/subq-ai-explained) | [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/05/subquadratic-launches-29m-bring-12m-token-context-windows-ai/))

### EU Agrees to Simplify AI Act Rules
On May 7, the EU Council and Parliament reached a political agreement on the AI Act simplification proposal (the "AI omnibus"), streamlining rules while adding a new provision prohibiting AI-generated non-consensual sexual and intimate content. Meanwhile in the U.S., with over 1,200 AI bills pending across states and no federal framework, the regulatory landscape remains fragmented. The Trump Administration is now reportedly reconsidering AI oversight positions it previously rejected, driven partly by Mythos's cyber capabilities. ([EU Consilium](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/07/artificial-intelligence-council-and-parliament-agree-to-simplify-and-streamline-rules/) | [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/15/ai-policy-patchwork-state-federal-regulation-framework-sonnenfeld-marcus/))
