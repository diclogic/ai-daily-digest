## Notable Papers & Research

### SubQ: First Commercial Subquadratic Frontier LLM
**Subquadratic Inc.** — Launched May 5 with $29M in seed funding and a 12-million-token context window. SubQ's Subquadratic Sparse Attention (SSA) architecture achieves roughly linear scaling, running ~52x faster than FlashAttention at 1M tokens. On the RULER 128K benchmark it scores 95% accuracy at ~$8, compared to ~$2,600 for Claude Opus at 94%. A genuinely novel architecture play in a field dominated by incremental scaling.
[Source](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/05/subquadratic-launches-29m-bring-12m-token-context-windows-ai/)

### "Agentic AI Orchestration Should be Bayes-consistent"
A position paper from 30 researchers argues that while making LLMs themselves Bayesian is computationally prohibitive, the orchestration layer — the system deciding tool calls and resource allocation — should be grounded in Bayesian principles. Reflects the growing focus on agentic AI system design.
[Source](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

### NVIDIA Ising: AI Models for Quantum Computing
NVIDIA launched Ising, an open-source model family purpose-built to accelerate quantum error correction, delivering up to 2.5x faster and 3x more accurate decoding compared to traditional approaches.
[Source](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates)

## Key Opinions & Statements

### Andrej Karpathy → Joins Anthropic
In the biggest talent move of the year, OpenAI co-founder and former Tesla AI chief Andrej Karpathy announced on May 19 that he is joining Anthropic's pre-training team under Nick Joseph. He will launch a new team focused on using Claude itself to accelerate pre-training research — a signal that Anthropic believes AI-assisted research, not just raw compute, is the path to staying competitive.

> *"I think the next few years at the frontier of LLMs will be especially formative. I am very excited to join the team here and get back to R&D."* — Karpathy on X

He added that he remains "deeply passionate about education" and plans to return to that work "in time," suggesting Eureka Labs may continue in some form.
[Source](https://venturebeat.com/technology/andrej-karpathy-announces-hes-joining-anthropic) · [Source](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/19/anthropic-hires-openai-cofounder-andrej-karpathy-former-tesla-ai-lead.html)

### Demis Hassabis — "Foothills of the Singularity"

## Other Notable AI News

### Musk v. OpenAI: Jury Dismisses All Claims
A federal jury in Oakland took less than two hours on May 19 to unanimously reject all of Elon Musk's claims against OpenAI and Sam Altman, finding that Musk's $150B lawsuit was filed too late under the statute of limitations. The verdict does not resolve the underlying question of whether OpenAI betrayed its nonprofit mission, but effectively ends Musk's legal challenge.
[Source](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/18/nx-s1-5822366/musk-altman-openai-jury-verdict-claims-dismissed) · [Source](https://www.newsweek.com/musk-openai-lawsuit-verdict-altman-ai-trial-11964171)

### Meta Cuts 8,000 Jobs, Redirects Workforce to AI
Meta began notifying ~8,000 employees on May 20 in the first wave of a 10% workforce reduction, while cancelling 6,000 open requisitions (14,000 total headcount impact). Simultaneously, ~7,000 workers are being redirected into new AI-focused teams. The company is raising AI infrastructure spending to up to $145 billion in 2026, even as it posted record quarterly revenue of $56.3B.
[Source](https://thenextweb.com/news/meta-layoffs-may-2026-ai-restructuring-thousands) · [Source](https://techjournal.org/meta-layoffs-begin-8000-jobs-ai-spending)

### Vatican to Publish First AI Encyclical with Anthropic Co-Founder
Pope Leo XIV will present his first encyclical, *Magnifica Humanitas* ("Magnificent Humanity"), on May 25 alongside Anthropic co-founder and interpretability researcher Christopher Olah. The document focuses on "the protection of the human person in the age of artificial intelligence." Leo signed it on May 15 — exactly 135 years after Pope Leo XIII signed *Rerum Novarum*, the landmark encyclical on labor and capitalism.
[Source](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/pope-leo-xiv-to-launch-his-first-encylical-a-document-on-artificial-intelligence-with-anthropics-co-founder) · [Source](https://www.vaticannews.va/en/pope/news/2026-05/pope-leo-xiv-first-encyclical-magnifica-humanitas.html)
