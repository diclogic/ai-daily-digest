## Notable Papers & Research

- **Google DeepMind AlphaEvolve One-Year Impact Report** — DeepMind published comprehensive results showing AlphaEvolve has graduated from pilot to core infrastructure. Key metrics: 0.7% of Google's global compute continuously recovered, 30% reduction in DNA variant detection errors via DeepConsensus, quantum circuit error reduced 10x on Willow processor, and AC Optimal Power Flow feasibility improved from 14% to 88% for electricity grids. Now deployed across infrastructure, genomics, quantum computing, and earth sciences. ([DeepMind Blog](https://deepmind.google/blog/alphaevolve-impact/))

- **SU-01: Gold-Medal Olympiad Reasoning** — Shanghai AI Laboratory's 30B-A3B model achieves gold-medal-level performance on IPhO 2024/2025 and IMO/USAMO 2025/2026, demonstrating that relatively small mixture-of-experts architectures can match elite human reasoning in physics and mathematics. ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current))

- **"LEMON: Learning Executable Multi-Agent Orchestration via Counterfactual Reinforcement Learning"** — Proposes a new framework for coordinating multiple AI agents using counterfactual RL, allowing agents to learn from alternative action paths rather than only observed outcomes. ([arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent))

- **"Thinking in Text and Images: Interleaved Vision-Language Reasoning Traces for Long-Horizon Robot Manipulation"** — Applies multimodal reasoning with interleaved text and image traces to enable robots to plan and execute complex manipulation tasks over extended time horizons. ([arXiv cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent))

### Mustafa Suleyman (Microsoft AI CEO)
Predicted that "most, if not all, professional tasks" in white-collar work will be fully automated by AI within 12–18 months. Speaking with the Financial Times, Suleyman said: "White-collar work where you're sitting down at a computer, either being a lawyer or an accountant or a project manager or a marketing person, most of those tasks will be fully automated by an AI." ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/article/why-microsoft-ai-chief-mustafa-suleyman-predicts-ai-automation-18-months/) | [Futurism](https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/microsoft-all-white-collar-tasks-automated))

### The Jevons Paradox Debate Continues
A Fortune article published May 17 reports that despite the AI boom, offshore call center employment is still skyrocketing — a real-world test case for the Jevons Paradox that Dario Amodei invoked earlier this month. The data suggests that AI-driven efficiency gains are expanding demand for services rather than eliminating jobs, at least in some sectors, lending some support to Amodei's shift away from his earlier "white-collar bloodbath" warnings. ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/17/ai-boom-cheap-offshore-call-center-labor-employment-jevons-paradox/))

## Other Notable AI News

### Musk v. Altman: Jury Deliberations Begin Monday
The nine-person advisory jury begins deliberating tomorrow after three weeks of testimony and closing arguments on May 14. At stake: whether Altman and Brockman misappropriated Musk's $38M donation when converting OpenAI to an $852B for-profit entity. Musk's legal team has asked the judge to remove Altman and Brockman from their roles and unwind the 2025 recapitalization. In an unusual move, the damages phase begins concurrently with liability deliberations. Musk notably skipped closing arguments to attend meetings in Beijing. Judge Gonzalez Rogers will make the final ruling based on the jury's advisory verdict. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/14/closing-arguments-jury-openai-musk-altman.html) | [MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/05/15/1137357/musk-v-altman-week-3/) | [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/musk-openai-closing-arguments-jury-deliberations))

### EU AI Omnibus: Compliance Deadlines Extended, Nudification AI Banned
The European Parliament and Council reached a provisional agreement on May 7 to simplify the EU AI Act. High-risk AI compliance deadlines pushed to December 2027 (stand-alone systems) and August 2028 (embedded in regulated products). SME thresholds expanded to 750 employees / €150M revenue. A new prohibition on AI-generated non-consensual intimate content (including nudification tools) takes effect December 2026. Formal adoption expected before August 2, 2026. ([EU Council](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/07/artificial-intelligence-council-and-parliament-agree-to-simplify-and-streamline-rules/) | [White & Case](https://www.whitecase.com/insight-alert/eu-agrees-digital-omnibus-deal-simplify-ai-rules))
