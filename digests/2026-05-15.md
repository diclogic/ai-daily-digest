## Notable Papers & Research

### DINOv3 — Self-Supervised Vision at Scale (Meta AI)
Meta AI released DINOv3, scaling self-supervised learning to 7B-parameter vision models trained on 1.7B images. For the first time, a single frozen SSL backbone outperforms weakly-supervised counterparts across object detection, semantic segmentation, and other dense prediction tasks. The key innovation is *Gram anchoring*, which prevents dense feature map degradation during long training schedules.
[Paper (arXiv)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.10104) · [Meta AI Blog](https://ai.meta.com/blog/dinov3-self-supervised-vision-model/)

### SubQ 1M-Preview — First Fully Subquadratic Frontier LLM (Subquadratic)
Miami-based startup Subquadratic emerged from stealth with a $29M seed round and a bold claim: a frontier LLM that is *not* a transformer. SubQ uses content-based sparse attention that scales linearly with context length, offering a native 12M-token context window at roughly 1/5 the cost of comparable models. Early benchmarks are impressive (97% on RULER 128K, 83 on MRCR v2 vs. Opus's 78), but independent verification is pending. As one commentator put it: "SubQ is either the biggest breakthrough since the Transformer... or it's AI Theranos."
[Subquadratic](https://subq.ai/introducing-subq) · [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/miami-startup-subquadratic-claims-1-000x-ai-efficiency-gain-with-subq-model-researchers-demand-independent-proof)

Anthropic's most capable model to date remains restricted under **Project Glasswing**, a coalition of ~40 organizations (AWS, Apple, Microsoft, Google, CrowdStrike, Palo Alto Networks, and others) using the model to find and patch vulnerabilities before public release.
